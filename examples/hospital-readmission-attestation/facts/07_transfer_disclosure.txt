FACT 07 — TRANSFER DISCLOSURE (FICTIONAL)

This fact freezes the hospital's patient transfer counts for April
2026, broken out by (a) direction (transfers OUT vs. transfers IN),
(b) within-system vs. independent receiving / sending facility, and
(c) primary reason category. PER REFUSAL RULE R8, the within-system
flag is mandatory; a transfer count without the within-system
breakdown obscures the within-system care-shifting pattern
documented in multi-facility health systems.

WHY THIS FACT IS LOAD-BEARING

Under CMS HRRP specifications, a patient transferred OUT of an
acute-care hospital to another acute-care facility may, depending
on the specific spec version, have the index-admission attribution
moved to the RECEIVING hospital — removing the originating
hospital from the readmission denominator for that case. The
specifics vary by HRRP measure year and by transfer-direction
clinical rationale. Per FACT 08, the planned-readmission and
transfer-handling rules in effect for this period are declared.
Per FACT 07, the actual transfer counts and patterns are sealed
so that the joint reading (spec + counts) is publicly diffable.

A separate concern: within-system transfers (to a sister facility
in the same health system) can functionally route a patient's
post-acute care to a related entity in ways that do not appear
as a readmission while still constituting continued care at the
system level. The within-system flag (mandatory per refusal rule
R8) makes this pattern publicly visible.

30-DAY READMISSION ATTRIBUTION FOR TRANSFERS

  Of the 58 patients transferred OUT in April 2026:
    Number subsequently re-admitted within 30 days of original
    index admission discharge (to ANY hospital, including the
    receiving transfer facility):                       7

  Per the FACT 08 spec, the readmission attribution for these
  cases is governed by the CMS transfer-handling rule: in this
  example we assume the standard FY2026 rule that attributes
  the index admission to the LAST acute-care hospital in the
  transfer chain (so 5 of these 7 are attributed to the
  receiving facility, NOT this hospital). 2 of the 7 are
  attributed to this hospital and ARE counted in FACT 04's
  numerator.

  This attribution rule is the kind of methodology detail that
  silently changes between HRRP measure years. By sealing both
  the rule (FACT 08) and the counts (FACT 07), a future
  attribution-rule change is publicly diffable: the analyst
  can re-apply the new rule to the sealed counts and see what
  the rate WOULD have been under the prior rule.

This fact asserts only the transfer counts, within-system flags,
reason categories, and 30-day attribution counts as recorded at
the April 2026 data freeze. It does not assert that any specific
transfer was clinically appropriate or inappropriate (per-case
clinical review is out of scope), that the within-system flag is
a flag for impropriety (it is not — many within-system transfers
are appropriate care), or that the CMS attribution rule applied
is the right rule (the rule itself is policy and contestable).
