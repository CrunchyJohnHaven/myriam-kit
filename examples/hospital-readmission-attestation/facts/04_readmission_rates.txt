FACT 04 — 30-DAY READMISSION RATES (FICTIONAL)

This fact reports the hospital's 30-day all-cause readmission rates
for the April 2026 attestation period (FACT 03), broken out by the
six HRRP-eligible conditions plus a hospital-wide (HWR) line. PER
REFUSAL RULE R3, raw rates appear alongside risk-adjusted rates;
risk-adjusted-only reporting is not permitted by this kit. PER
REFUSAL RULE R4, per-condition breakdown is the load-bearing
content; the hospital-wide line is presented only IN ADDITION to
the per-condition lines.

NOTES ON THE TABLE

  - "Raw numerator" is the count of unique index admissions in the
    condition cohort that had at least one all-cause inpatient
    readmission to ANY hospital within 30 days of discharge from
    the index admission. (Readmissions to other hospitals are
    captured per the CMS spec's master-beneficiary-summary linkage;
    a hospital-internal-only count would be biased low.)
  - "Raw denominator" is the count of index admissions for the
    condition cohort during the period, after applying the standard
    HRRP exclusion criteria enumerated in FACT 08.
  - "Raw rate" is numerator / denominator. The 95% confidence
    interval is computed by the Wilson score interval method (not
    Wald), which is more reliable than Wald for small denominators
    common at the per-condition per-month level.
  - "Risk-adjusted rate" is the CMS hierarchical-logistic-regression
    estimate per the FY2026 specification, with 95% CI from the
    spec's standard estimator. The risk-adjusted rate uses the
    multi-year calibration window from the specification, NOT a
    single-month estimate; reading month-to-month variation in the
    risk-adjusted column should be interpreted with the multi-year
    calibration in mind. Raw rates (left columns) are the
    appropriate signal for month-over-month diffing.

INTERPRETATION CAUTION

  Per the README "what this kit DOES NOT do" section: a high or
  low rate above does not by itself indicate good or bad quality
  of care. The rate reflects the joint outcome of admission case
  mix, discharge planning, post-discharge community resources,
  patient adherence, and many other factors. The seal anchors the
  RECORD of the rates; it does not adjudicate their causes. The
  seal also does not capture 30-day post-discharge mortality
  (which is sealed in a separate companion attestation per
  refusal rule R6 — that companion's Merkle root is referenced
  in FACT 08).

CONFIDENCE-INTERVAL READING GUIDANCE

  Per-condition per-month CIs are wide, especially for AMI and
  CABG, because the per-month per-condition denominators at a
  single hospital are small. A single-month CI substantially
  overlapping another hospital's single-month CI does NOT
  indicate the hospitals are different in their underlying rates.
  Multi-month rolling rates (computed by the consumer of the seal
  by averaging across sealed monthly publications, NOT by the
  hospital) are the appropriate unit for cross-hospital
  comparison.

This fact asserts only the readmission counts and rates as recorded
at the April 2026 data freeze. It does not assert that the
readmissions were avoidable, that the rates reflect care quality,
that the risk-adjustment model is correctly specified for this
hospital's case mix, that any single-month rate is comparable to
any other hospital's single-month rate, or that the absence of a
readmission count in any cell means a patient was not in need of
re-admission (some unmet need is invisible to the readmission
measure entirely).
