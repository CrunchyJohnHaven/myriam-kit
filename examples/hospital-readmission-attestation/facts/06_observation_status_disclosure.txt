FACT 06 — OBSERVATION-STATUS DISCLOSURE (FICTIONAL)

This fact freezes the hospital's observation-status discharge counts,
the justification category distribution, the duration distribution,
and the inpatient-to-observation conversion count for April 2026.
PER REFUSAL RULE R7, all these breakdowns are mandatory; an
observation count without category and duration breakdown is
exactly the gaming-permissive aggregate the seal is designed to
prevent.

WHY THIS FACT IS LOAD-BEARING

The HRRP measure counts INPATIENT readmissions of INPATIENT index
admissions. An admission decision to use OBSERVATION STATUS instead
of INPATIENT STATUS removes the encounter from the HRRP denominator
entirely. The peer-reviewed literature (Sheehy et al., JAMA Internal
Medicine 2013; Zuckerman et al., NEJM 2016 and 2017 follow-ups;
Wadhera et al., JAMA 2018) documents substantial national growth in
observation-status discharges across the HRRP era. Some of that
growth is appropriate clinical practice (the "two-midnight rule" was
intended to clarify the inpatient-vs-observation boundary); some is
documented to be HRRP-incentive-driven. Per-hospital monthly sealed
observation counts make each individual hospital's contribution to
the pattern publicly diffable.

CROSS-WALK NOTE FOR DIFFING ACROSS MONTHS

When the next month's FACT 06 is sealed, the consumer of the
seals can compare:

  - Total observation count (April 287 → May ?)
  - Justification category mix (chest pain r/o proportion, etc.)
  - Duration distribution (especially the >48-hour bucket)
  - Inpatient-to-observation conversion direction and magnitude
  - Post-observation 30-day re-encounter rate

Any anomalous month-over-month movement in these is publicly
visible. The kit does NOT adjudicate whether the movement is
gaming or genuine practice change; it makes the movement
visible.

This fact asserts only the observation-status discharge counts and
breakdowns as recorded at the April 2026 data freeze. It does not
assert that any specific observation classification was clinically
appropriate or inappropriate (per-case clinical review is out of
scope), that the justification category labels are exhaustive of
all clinical scenarios, or that observation-vs-inpatient
classification follows a single bright line in every case (the
two-midnight rule has known interpretation gray areas).
