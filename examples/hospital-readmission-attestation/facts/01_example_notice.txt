FACT 01 — EXAMPLE NOTICE

This publication is an EXAMPLE demonstration of how a hospital, a
hospital system Chief Medical Officer + Quality officer pair, a state
hospital association, or a public-health transparency program can use
myriam-kit to seal a monthly 30-day all-cause readmission attestation
on top of the existing CMS Hospital Readmissions Reduction Program
(HRRP) reporting workflow. The hospital name, the CMS Certification
Number, the certifying officers, the attestation period, the per-
condition counts and rates, the observation and transfer disclosures,
and the methodology version are ALL FICTIONAL.

The purpose: show the SHAPE of a real hospital readmission attestation
seal. A real attestation would replace each fact body with the
hospital's actual monthly data, under the constraints documented in
the README (no per-patient identifiers, no per-physician attribution,
raw rates always alongside risk-adjusted rates, per-condition
breakdown always alongside any hospital-wide rate, observation status
and transfer disclosures always present, methodology change from
prior period always declared).

This kit is intended to compose with — not replace — existing hospital
quality reporting frameworks (CMS HRRP, CMS HIQR, CMS Hospital
Compare / Care Compare, state-level hospital reporting programs, the
Joint Commission accreditation, internal peer review under the
Healthcare Quality Improvement Act). The MYRIAM seal provides
Bitcoin-anchored tamper-evidence on top of whatever HRRP submission,
internal quality committee, and statutory reporting record already
exists under those frameworks.

The seal does NOT certify that the hospital provides good care. It
commits that the readmission-record and methodology had THIS exact
byte content at the moment of monthly attestation. The seal's value
is in being diffable against the next month's seal: a month-over-
month change in observation-status counts, transfer patterns, case
mix, or methodology becomes publicly detectable in a way that single-
period reporting does not enable.

This fact asserts only that the publication is an example, not a real
hospital readmission attestation. No real hospital is hereby attested
to. No real CMO, Quality officer, physician, or patient is hereby
identified.
