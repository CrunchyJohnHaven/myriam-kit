RATIONALE FOR ROLE-LEVEL (NOT PERSON-LEVEL) PUBLICATION

  Public attestation of named individuals creates a retaliation
  surface against the people most likely to honestly attest to
  problematic numbers. Role-level publication (CMO, Quality VP)
  preserves accountability while leaving person-level binding to a
  separately-held company record producible under regulator
  subpoena, OIG demand, or accreditation surveyor request. The
  person-holding-the-role for this attestation period is recorded
  in an internal record; the SHA-256 of that internal record
  appears in FACT 08, so the binding is hashable but not publicly
  re-identifiable.

This fact binds the entire sealed publication to a named, accountable
hospital and to the two role-level offices responsible for the
attestation. Naming the offices is not a violation of the refusal
gates (offices are public roles, not private third parties); the
seal makes the hospital's attestation publicly verifiable against
the office, not against any individual.

This fact asserts only the hospital identification and certifying-
office identification fields for the fictional hospital. It does not
assert that any real hospital or CMS Certification Number exists, nor
that the fictional offices reflect any actual institution. It does
not assert anything about the quality of care delivered at any real
hospital.
