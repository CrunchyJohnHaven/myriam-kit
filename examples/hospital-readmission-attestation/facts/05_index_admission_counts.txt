FACT 05 — INDEX ADMISSION COUNTS AND CASE-MIX BREAKDOWN (FICTIONAL)

This fact reports the denominators that feed FACT 04 — the index
admission counts per condition AND the total inpatient discharge
count for the period AND the payer-mix breakdown so case-mix shifts
between months are publicly diffable. PER REFUSAL RULE R4, this
breakdown is mandatory; a rate without a denominator + payer mix
hides the most important gaming vector (silent case-mix shift away
from high-readmission conditions and toward low-readmission
conditions).

WHY THE PAYER BREAKDOWN MATTERS FOR DIFFING

  HRRP applies to Medicare fee-for-service admissions. Hospitals
  with declining Medicare FFS share (and growing Medicare
  Advantage share) can see apparent HRRP-rate improvements that
  reflect denominator composition change, not care change. A
  month-over-month silent shift in the Medicare FFS percentage
  is publicly visible across sealed publications. The seal does
  not adjudicate whether such a shift reflects market dynamics,
  hospital admission policy, or anything else; it makes the shift
  visible.

  Hospitals with growing Medicaid and self-pay share often serve
  populations with documented higher readmission risk under
  standard CMS risk adjustment, which contributes to the
  documented HRRP penalty disparity affecting safety-net
  hospitals (Khera et al., JAMA 2018, and follow-on literature).
  A sealed payer mix allows independent researchers and the
  public to interpret rate changes in context.

INDEX-VS-OBSERVATION ADMISSION BOUNDARY

  Patients discharged from the emergency department, or admitted
  under "observation status" rather than inpatient status, are
  NOT counted as index admissions for HRRP-cohort denominators
  above. Observation-status counts are reported separately in
  FACT 06 to make the inpatient-vs-observation boundary publicly
  diffable. Inpatient-status-to-observation-status shifts that
  silently move patients OUT of the HRRP denominator are exactly
  the gaming vector FACT 06 is designed to make visible.

This fact asserts only the index admission counts, exclusion counts,
total inpatient discharge count, and payer-mix breakdown as recorded
at the April 2026 data freeze. It does not assert that the case mix
is representative of any other hospital, that the exclusion criteria
are correctly applied in every individual case (per-case audit is
out of scope), or that the payer breakdown is normative in any way.
