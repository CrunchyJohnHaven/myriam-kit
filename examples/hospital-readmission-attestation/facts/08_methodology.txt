DENOMINATOR INCLUSION CRITERIA (PER CMS SPEC)

  Index admissions are included if:
    - Inpatient acute-care hospital admission (not observation,
      not ED-only)
    - Primary discharge diagnosis matches one of the HRRP
      condition cohorts (AMI, HF, PN, COPD, CABG, THA/TKA per
      ICD-10-CM / ICD-10-PCS codes in the spec's value-set tables)
    - Patient age 18 years or older
    - Patient enrolled in Medicare FFS Part A for the 12 months
      prior to the index admission AND for 30 days post-discharge
      (continuous-enrollment requirement; Medicare Advantage and
      other coverage types are not in the HRRP denominator)
    - Discharge alive (in-hospital deaths excluded)

EXCLUSION CRITERIA APPLIED (PER CMS SPEC; ITEMIZED COUNTS FROM
FACT 05)

  Excluded if:
    - Patient transferred IN to the index admission from another
      acute-care facility (transfer-in attribution: the index
      goes to the SENDING hospital)
    - Patient discharged Against Medical Advice (LAMA)
    - Patient died during the index admission (in-hospital death;
      see "discharge alive" inclusion above)
    - Patient enrolled in Medicare hospice during the 30-day
      window
    - For cohort-specific cases per the spec: certain
      cohort-specific exclusions (e.g. AMI patients who left
      against medical advice or were transferred to a more
      specialized facility)

  Per-cohort exclusion counts: see FACT 05 for the itemized
  exclusion counts per cohort.

PLANNED-READMISSION EXCLUSION HANDLING

  Per the CMS planned-readmission algorithm (Horwitz et al.;
  CMS-adopted), readmissions for planned procedures (e.g.
  scheduled chemotherapy, planned coronary revascularization,
  planned post-discharge surgical procedures) are EXCLUDED from
  the numerator of FACT 04. The April 2026 counts in FACT 04
  reflect the CMS planned-readmission algorithm version v4.0
  as published in the spec.

  Planned-readmission exclusion count for April 2026:        7
  (these 7 readmissions WOULD have been counted in the FACT 04
  numerator absent the planned-readmission exclusion; they are
  excluded from the numerator per the spec algorithm)

  The planned-readmission algorithm is a known source of inter-
  hospital methodology variation. The sealed count of planned-
  excluded readmissions makes it possible for an external
  analyst to add the planned exclusions back and compute
  an "all-readmission" rate for cross-comparison.

TRANSFER-OUT ATTRIBUTION RULE

  Per the FY2026 CMS HRRP spec, for patients transferred OUT to
  another acute-care facility during the index admission:
    - The index admission is attributed to the LAST acute-care
      hospital in the transfer chain.
    - If this hospital is the LAST in the chain, the index
      admission appears in this hospital's FACT 04 denominator.
    - If this hospital is NOT the last in the chain (the
      patient was transferred onward to another acute-care
      facility), the index admission is attributed to the
      receiving / final facility, and does NOT appear in this
      hospital's denominator.

  For the April 2026 period: of the 58 patients transferred OUT
  (FACT 07), 5 were attributed under this rule to receiving
  facilities and do NOT appear in FACT 04 denominators or
  numerators for this hospital; 2 were attributed to this
  hospital and DO appear (as readmissions within 30 days of
  this hospital's discharge), per FACT 07.

OBSERVATION-STATUS-AS-INDEX-ADMISSION POLICY

  Per CMS HRRP spec: observation status discharges are NOT
  index admissions. The 287 observation discharges in FACT 06
  are NOT counted in FACT 04 or FACT 05 HRRP denominators.

  This policy is unchanged between FY2025 and FY2026; any
  future CMS change to this policy will be disclosed in the
  FACT 08 of the first attestation after the change, with a
  reference to this period's Merkle root.
