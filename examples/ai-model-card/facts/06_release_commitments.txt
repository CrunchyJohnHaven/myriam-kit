FACT 06 — PRE-DEPLOYMENT COMMITMENTS + SIGNING PRINCIPAL

In the spirit of the MYRIAM protocol's "minimize preventable deaths over
time" objective function, the publisher commits — in advance of release
— to the following:

  1. NO SILENT FINE-TUNING. The checkpoint identified by the weights
     hash in FACT 03 will not be silently fine-tuned and redistributed
     under the same name. Any fine-tune produces a new model name, a
     new MYRIAM publication with a new weights hash, and the predecessor
     remains the canonical "Calm-mini-v0."

  2. NO SILENT EVAL UPDATES. If new evals are run and reported, they
     are sealed in a new MYRIAM publication that references this one
     by Merkle root. Eval-score revisions are versioned, not overwritten.

  3. KNOWN FAILURE MODES MAY GROW, NEVER SHRINK. Discovery of new
     failure modes triggers a new sealed publication adding to the
     list in FACT 04. Removal of a known failure mode from the list
     requires evidence that the failure mode has been actually fixed
     (which, for a frozen checkpoint, is structurally impossible —
     so the list is effectively monotonic).

  4. TAKEDOWN HANDLING. If the publisher is compelled to remove the
     model from distribution, the publisher commits to publishing a
     final sealed MYRIAM publication stating the takedown event and,
     where legally permitted, the reason.
