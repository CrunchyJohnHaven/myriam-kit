This page is a sealed publication of 6 facts that, together, constitute a complete release attestation for an AI model: identification, weights hash, model card, eval scores, and pre-deployment commitments. The 6 facts have been hashed (SHA-256), combined into a Bitcoin-style Merkle root, and committed to the Bitcoin blockchain via OpenTimestamps against two independent calendar operators. Any change to any fact would produce a Merkle root different from the one committed to Bitcoin, and would therefore be detectable.

What this example is for

This publication exists to demonstrate that an AI model release can be sealed under cryptographic protocol such that:

  • The weights file's hash is publicly committed at release time, making post-release silent tampering with the distributed weights detectable.
  • The model card's exact text is publicly committed, making future "we never claimed that" arguments harder.
  • The eval results at release are publicly committed, making model capability drift over time empirically checkable.
  • The pre-deployment commitments are publicly committed, making future deviations from those commitments publicly detectable.

No real model is hereby released. To use this pattern for a real release, fork myriam-kit, scaffold a new publication, replace the facts in this example with your real artifacts, and seal.
