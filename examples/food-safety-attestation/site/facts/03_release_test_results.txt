CRITICAL INTERPRETATION CONSTRAINT

The sample-based PASS results above attest to what the sampled
analytical units tested ND or within-spec for. Microbial contamination
in bulk food product is HETEROGENEOUS — Listeria, Salmonella, and
STEC pathogens occur in low-prevalence localized hotspots, not
uniformly distributed through a lot. Standard ICMSF sampling plans
have a documented and bounded probability of detecting contamination
present at low prevalence; the probability is NOT 1. A lot that
passes its release sampling can still contain contaminated units that
were not sampled. The 2008-onward peanut-product Salmonella outbreaks,
the 2011 European STEC O104:H4 sprouted-seed outbreak, the 2015 ice-
cream Listeria monocytogenes outbreak, the 2018 leafy-greens
multistate STEC O157:H7 outbreaks, and the 2008 melamine adulteration
of infant formula in China each illustrate failure modes the release
sampling either missed (heterogeneous contamination, novel hazard,
adulteration not screened for) or could not have detected (deliberate
fraudulent substitution outside the assay set). The seal anchors
WHICH samples returned WHICH results at release. It does not
extrapolate from samples to the safety of every consumer unit in the
lot. That inference is a separate epistemological step, governed by
the validated sampling plan, the HACCP hazard analysis, and post-
release surveillance.

This fact asserts only the release-testing and environmental-
monitoring manifest above for the fictional lot at the moment of
release. It does not assert that the tests above are exhaustive of
all tests required by any specific regulatory regime, that the
sampling plan's confidence level guarantees absence of pathogens in
all consumer units, that PASS results entail consumer safety in any
specific population (including immunocompromised, pregnant, infant,
or elderly consumers for whom Listeria and Salmonella present
elevated risk), or that contamination introduced AFTER release (in
distribution, retail handling, or consumer preparation) is anchored
by this seal.
