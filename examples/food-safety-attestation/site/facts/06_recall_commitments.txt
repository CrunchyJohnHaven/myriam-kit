FACT 06 — RECALL AND POST-MARKET SURVEILLANCE COMMITMENTS (FICTIONAL)

In the spirit of the MYRIAM protocol's "minimize preventable deaths
over time" objective function, and as the food-safety analog of
pharmacovigilance, the fictional manufacturer commits at the moment
of release to the following. These commitments are NOT a substitute
for the statutory recall obligations under FSMA, FSIS recall policy,
EU Regulation (EC) 178/2002 Article 19, or any other applicable
regime — they are an ADDITIVE, publicly comparable layer that makes
selective compliance detectable.

  1. ILLNESS-SIGNAL TRANSPARENCY

     Any complaint of foodborne illness causally associated by the
     manufacturer's preliminary investigation with lot
     FFM-2026-W19-L042 will be reported to FSIS / FDA / state public-
     health authorities within statutorily required timelines AND an
     aggregate complaint summary will be published in a NEW sealed
     MYRIAM publication (referencing this one by Merkle root) at the
     30-day and 90-day post-release marks IF any signal is detected.
     If no signal is detected, the 90-day "no-signal" attestation is
     also sealed and published, so silent suppression of a known
     signal is detectable.

  2. RECALL EXECUTION

     If a Class I recall trigger is identified for this lot (per FDA
     21 CFR 7.3(m) or FSIS Directive 8080.1 definition: "reasonable
     probability that use will cause serious adverse health
     consequences or death"), the manufacturer commits to:
       - Notifying the appropriate regulator within 24 hours of
         internal Class I determination
       - Initiating recall within 72 hours of regulator concurrence
       - Publishing the recall scope (which lot codes, distribution
         destinations, dose / serving ranges) as a NEW sealed
         MYRIAM publication
       - Maintaining recall-completeness reporting until at least
         98 percent of distributed units are accounted for, with
         monthly sealed updates until completion
       - For unaccounted units beyond 98 percent: sealed attestation
         of the unaccounted residual with consumer-facing public
         notice

     The 72-hour and 98-percent commitments above are tighter than the
     statutory minimums in some jurisdictions. They are voluntary; the
     seal commits them publicly. A future failure to meet these self-
     imposed limits is publicly comparable against this declaration.

  3. SUPPLY-CHAIN CONTAMINATION SCENARIOS

     If contamination is traced upstream to a specific supplier
     ingredient lot, the manufacturer commits to:
       - Quarantining all unfilled production using that ingredient lot
       - Issuing a supplier-traceback advisory through FDA Reportable
         Food Registry (or successor) within 24 hours of confirmation
       - Sealing the traceback timeline as a NEW MYRIAM publication
         (with supplier identity withheld pending litigation /
         confidentiality resolution but the contaminated-ingredient
         lot code published)

  4. NO SILENT RETESTING

     If retesting of any release test in FACT 03 is performed after
     this release, the retest result will be published as a NEW
     sealed MYRIAM publication regardless of result. Replacement of
     a sealed PASS with an unsealed retest result is NOT a legitimate
     amendment under this commitment.

  5. NO POST-HOC SPECIFICATION CHANGES

     If the release-specification limits in FACT 03 are revised by
     the manufacturer or regulator after this release (e.g. method
     replaced, LOD changed, sampling plan updated), the revised
     limits will be sealed in a NEW publication. The original sealed
     limits remain the basis on which this lot was released.

  6. AGGREGATE SAFETY-SIGNAL SHARING

     Anonymized aggregate complaint and surveillance data for this
     product CATEGORY (RTE cooked chicken) will be contributed to
     industry-wide aggregation programs (IFSAC, CDC FoodNet) per
     existing protocols. The contribution itself is not sealed here,
     but acknowledgment that the manufacturer participates in those
     programs is.

ANALOG TO PHARMACOVIGILANCE

In pharmaceuticals, post-market surveillance is a regulated discipline
(VAERS, EudraVigilance, ICH E2D). In food, the analog is more
fragmented: CDC PulseNet for pathogen whole-genome sequencing,
FoodNet for active surveillance, FSIS and FDA for trace-back, RFR for
manufacturer self-reporting. The manufacturer's role in this analog
is contributing reliable lot-level identifiers, prompt internal-
signal escalation, and recall execution capacity. The commitments
above are the food-safety equivalent of a manufacturer's
pharmacovigilance plan at the moment of release.

This fact asserts only the recall and surveillance commitments as
declared. It does not assert that the commitments will be honored in
practice, that honoring them is sufficient for any specific safety
guarantee, or that the manufacturer has the operational capacity to
execute a Class I recall under all foreseeable distribution scenarios
(an unlikely but possible failure mode that defense-of-recall-
capability programs and FSIS / FDA recall exercises are designed to
detect). The commitments are a forensic anchor. Any future deviation
becomes publicly comparable against this sealed declaration.
