This page is a sealed publication of 8 facts that together constitute a complete food-safety release seal: an example notice, product and batch identification, release-testing results, methodology and lab qualification, release decision, recall and post-market surveillance commitments, companion disclosures and refusal record, and signing principal. The 8 facts have been hashed (SHA-256), combined into a Bitcoin-style Merkle root, and committed to the Bitcoin blockchain via OpenTimestamps against two independent calendar operators. Any change to any fact would produce a different Merkle root and be detectable by any member of the public.

CRITICAL INTERPRETATION CONSTRAINT The sample-based PASS results above attest to what the sampled analytical units tested ND or within-spec for. Microbial contamination in bulk food product is HETEROGENEOUS — Listeria, Salmonella, and STEC pathogens occur in low-prevalence localized hotspots, not uniformly distributed through a lot. Standard ICMSF sampling plans have a documented and bounded probability of detecting contamination present at low prevalence; the probability is NOT 1. A lot that passes its release sampling can still contain contaminated units that were not sampled. The 2008-onward peanut-product Salmonella outbreaks, the 2011 European STEC O104:H4 sprouted-seed outbreak, the 2015 ice- cream Listeria monocytogenes outbreak, the 2018 leafy-greens multistate STEC O157:H7 outbreaks, and the 2008 melamine adulteration of infant formula in China each illustrate failure modes the release sampling either missed (heterogeneous contamination, novel hazard, adulteration not screened for) or could not have detected (deliberate fraudulent substitution outside the assay set). The seal anchors WHICH samples returned WHICH results at release. It does not extrapolate from samples to the safety of every consumer unit in the lot. That inference is a separate epistemological step, governed by the validated sampling plan, the HACCP hazard analysis, and post- release surveillance. This fact asserts only the release-testing and environmental- monitoring manifest above for the fictional lot at the moment of release. It does not assert that the tests above are exhaustive of all tests required by any specific regulatory regime, that the sampling plan's confidence level guarantees absence of pathogens in all consumer units, that PASS results entail consumer safety in any specific population (including immunocompromised, pregnant, infant, or elderly consumers for whom Listeria and Salmonella present elevated risk), or that contamination introduced AFTER release (in distribution, retail handling, or consumer preparation) is anchored by this seal.

What this protocol promises (and doesn't)

The protocol delivers detectability of tampering with the release record, not prevention of contamination. It delivers content-addressed mirroring at the IPFS layer, but not guaranteed long-term pinning durability without additional pin redundancy. It does not assert that any sealed fact is true; it asserts only that the fact was published in its current bytewise form at the sealed time.

Specifically and critically:

  • The seal does NOT prevent contamination.
  • The seal does NOT guarantee all units in a passing lot are safe (microbial contamination is heterogeneous; sampling has bounded detection probability).
  • The seal does NOT detect contemporaneous fraud at the lab or QA-decision layer.
  • The seal does NOT replace FSMA / FSIS / EU competent-authority records.
  • The seal does NOT make a population-specific safety claim (immunocompromised, pregnant, infant, elderly consumers face documented elevated risk from Listeria / Salmonella even at sub-detection-limit concentrations).
