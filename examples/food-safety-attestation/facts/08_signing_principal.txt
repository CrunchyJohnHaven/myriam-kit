WHY THE PRINCIPAL IS ROLE-LEVEL, NOT PERSON-LEVEL

In real-world food-safety practice, the binding between a release
decision and the individual signing it is established through:

  - the company's contemporaneous QA records, retained per FSMA /
    FSIS recordkeeping requirements (at least 2 years for human
    food, 3 years for non-shelf-stable RTE under FSMA),
  - the company's HR records of role assignment (who held the
    QA Director role on 2026-05-12),
  - and the individual's testimony or affidavit in any subsequent
    regulatory or litigation proceeding.

A public Bitcoin-anchored seal is not the appropriate venue to bind
a worker's personal identity to a food-safety decision. That binding
belongs in the company's controlled records, where access is governed
by employment privacy law, by FSMA Section 402 whistleblower
protections, and by general worker-safety considerations against
harassment. The role-level public seal plus the separately-held
role-to-individual binding (hashed in FACT 07) is the kit's default;
companies in jurisdictions where public personal-naming is required
(e.g. some EU member-state competent authorities require a named
"qualified person" on the public label) can amend their fork to
include the personal name, but the default refusal favors worker
safety. See refusal-rule R1 in FACT 07 and in the README.

In a real attestation, the signing principal section should include:

  - The manufacturer's legal name and regulatory establishment ID.
  - The role and counter-sign role for each signatory.
  - A cryptographic key (PGP, X.509, role-issued) so that future
    statements claimed to be from the signing principal can be
    cryptographically verified against this seal.
  - The release and sealing timestamps.
  - A canonical contact channel for regulator coordination and
    recall response.

The signing principal section SHOULD NOT include:

  - Personal names, contact information, or home addresses of QA
    staff, plant managers, line workers, or visiting inspectors.
  - Identifying information about workers' family members or
    employment history.
  - Precise facility coordinates beyond region.
  - Supplier identities (per refusal-rule R4) or customer
    identities (per refusal-rule R5).
  - Trade secrets about the manufacturing process beyond what FSMA
    or FSIS specifically require in the public-record portion of
    the food-safety plan.

This fact asserts only the signing principal identification fields
for the fictional lot release as declared. It does not assert any of
those fields about any real manufacturer, any real worker, or any
real food product.
