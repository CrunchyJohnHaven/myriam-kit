FACT 01 — EXAMPLE NOTICE

This publication is an EXAMPLE demonstration of how a food manufacturer,
a food-safety inspector, or a regulatory authority can use myriam-kit
to seal a food-safety attestation at the moment of batch release or
at sign-off of an inspection report. The manufacturer name, facility
identifiers, lot numbers, testing laboratories, methods, numerical
results, and all personnel are FICTIONAL.

The purpose: show the SHAPE of a real food-safety release seal. A real
attestation would replace each fact body with the actual batch's
release-testing records, methodology, and release decision — under the
constraints documented in the README (PII refusals, supply-chain
confidentiality boundaries, etc.).

This kit is intended to compose with — not replace — existing food-
safety regulatory frameworks (FDA FSMA, USDA FSIS, EU Regulation
(EC) 178/2002 and the Hygiene Package, Codex Alimentarius / HACCP,
ISO 22000, FSSC 22000, BRCGS, SQF). The MYRIAM seal provides Bitcoin-
anchored tamper-evidence on top of whatever HACCP plan, inspection
record, or release decision already exists under that regulatory
framework.

The seal does NOT certify that the released batch is safe. It commits
that the testing-and-decision record had THIS exact byte content at
the moment of release. The distinction is load-bearing and is
elaborated in the README and in FACT 03's closing disclaimer.

This fact asserts only that the publication is an example, not a real
batch release. No real batch is hereby released. No real manufacturer,
facility, laboratory, inspector, or food product is hereby named.
