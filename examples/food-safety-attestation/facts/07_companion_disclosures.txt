records what the kit explicitly refused to seal and any companion
disclosures the manufacturer is obliged to publish alongside the
release record so that selective omission is detectable. The
disclosures here are not optional — leaving any of them blank without
explicit "none at release time" attestation is itself a public red
flag.

REFUSAL RECORD — patterns the kit refused to seal

The kit does NOT seal the following types of facts, even if requested
by the manufacturer, a customer QA team, a third-party certifier, or
a co-equal authority:

  R1. ANY PII OF WORKERS, SUPERVISORS, OR INSPECTORS

      No fact may include personal names, employee ID numbers, badge
      numbers, social security numbers, home addresses, personal
      phone numbers, or any other identifier of individual food-
      production workers, supervisors, QA staff, or visiting
      inspectors. Worker safety against retaliation and harassment
      is the load-bearing constraint here; food-safety attestation
      can be done at role-level without naming individuals.

  R2. PRECISE FACILITY LOCATION

      The facility region (state / multi-state region) is published
      in FACT 02. The precise street address, GPS coordinates, or
      any identifier of granularity sufficient to physically
      identify the specific plant on a map is NOT published. The
      regulatory establishment number, when published, is the
      regulator's handle for the facility; physical-security
      considerations and worker safety against targeted attacks
      argue against publishing a public address on Bitcoin.

  R3. INDIVIDUAL PRODUCTION-SHIFT PERSONNEL

      Shift schedules, individual worker rotations, line operator
      personal IDs — none of these appear in this seal. Pattern-of-
      life of individual workers must not be derivable from the
      public attestation.

  R4. SUPPLIER IDENTITIES THAT COMPROMISE CONTRACTS

      Supplier identities, supplier facility locations, supplier
      lot codes (except in the FACT 06 case where a contamination
      traceback REQUIRES naming a supplier's contaminated lot for
      public health) are NOT published. Many supply contracts
      contain confidentiality provisions; the seal does not breach
      those by default. Where a public-health emergency requires
      naming a supplier's contaminated lot, that disclosure is
      handled through the regulator's recall channels and is
      additionally sealed in a NEW MYRIAM publication, not by
      retroactive amendment to this one.

  R5. CUSTOMER LISTS AND DISTRIBUTION DESTINATIONS BEYOND HIGH-LEVEL

      The high-level regional summary in FACT 05 is published; the
      specific customer list (which retailers, which distribution
      centers, which foodservice accounts) is NOT. Customer
      identity is contractually confidential and competitively
      sensitive; the regulator obtains the customer list through
      established recall-coordination channels.

  R6. SAFETY CLAIMS OR LEGAL CONCLUSIONS

      The kit will not seal a fact body that claims the product is
      "safe," that it is "GRAS for this use" (GRAS is an FDA
      determination), that it meets "all applicable food safety
      standards" (this is a legal conclusion), or any other safety
      or legal conclusion. Safety claims are downstream of the
      facts the seal anchors; the kit anchors the testing record
      and the release decision, NOT a safety conclusion.

  R7. WORKER-COMPLAINT OR WHISTLEBLOWER PATTERNS

      The kit refuses to publish any pattern that could re-identify
      a worker who has filed an OSHA, USDA, or company-internal
      complaint about food-safety or workplace-safety practices.
      Such complainants are protected under FSMA Section 402
      (whistleblower protection) and parallel statutes; the seal
      must not become a re-identification vector. Whistleblower
      attestations have their own kit
      (examples/whistleblower-manifest in this same repository) with
      its own refusal-rule set tuned for that use case.

  R8. SEALING BY ANYONE OTHER THAN THE RELEASING MANUFACTURER'S
      AUTHORIZED PRINCIPAL

      A third-party certifier, customer QA team, or independent
      auditor may use the same kit to seal THEIR own independent
      verification or audit findings; but they may not seal the
      ORIGINAL release decision under the manufacturer's name. The
      principal in FACT 08 is the only valid signer for that.

If during release any request matching R1-R8 was made and refused,
that refusal would be documented as a sealed fact:
  "Refusal #N: party X requested sealing of [pattern]; the kit
  refused per refusal-rule R[m]."

  Refusals this release: NONE. (FICTIONAL)

This fact asserts only the companion-disclosure status and any
refusal record at the moment of release. It does not assert that the
refusal-rule list is exhaustive of all PII or sensitive-disclosure
patterns, that all companion disclosures have been identified by the
manufacturer (selective omission of a disclosure category not yet
contemplated is exactly the failure mode this fact is designed to
make detectable over time), or that the disclosures listed are
themselves non-contestable.
