This fact binds the entire sealed publication to a named, accountable
agency, a named incident, and two role-level offices responsible for
the attestation. Naming the agency and the offices is not a violation
of the refusal gates (the agency is a public body and the offices are
public roles, not private third parties); the seal makes the agency's
attestation publicly verifiable against the agency and the two
offices, not against any individual officer's personal name.

This fact asserts only the agency identification, incident
identification at intake-time classification, federal-monitoring
status at intake, BWC policy version in effect, and the role-level
identification of the two certifying officers. It does not assert:
  - that the incident was correctly classified (later re-
    classification would trigger an amendment seal per refusal rule
    R8),
  - that any officer's conduct was lawful, proper, within policy, or
    otherwise endorsed by the agency,
  - that the federal-monitoring status will remain unchanged,
  - that the BWC policy version in effect was followed in this
    incident (compliance with policy is a separate IA-review
    question),
  - that any named or unnamed individual is or is not a suspect,
    complainant, victim, witness, or third party.
