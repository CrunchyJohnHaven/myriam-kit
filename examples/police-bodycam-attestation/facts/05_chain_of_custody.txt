FACT 05 — CHAIN OF CUSTODY (FICTIONAL)

This fact records the custody events from device-side capture
completion through the completion of evidence-management-system
(DEMS) ingest. Custodians are identified by ROLE, NOT by personal
name. A separately-sealed companion record (hashed below) binds role
labels to the specific individuals on duty; that companion record is
held by the agency under standard discovery, oversight, and IA
process — not published in this public seal. The role-only
publication is the kit's default refusal rule R7 (see FACT 07) — it
prevents the public seal from creating a permanent searchable
identifier of every officer involved in every incident, which has in
past cases been used as a vector for harassment, retaliation against
whistleblower-prone officers, and doxxing.

PER REFUSAL RULE R6, any unexplained access gap in this log would
have triggered refusal-to-seal. The log below is presented as the
agency's contemporaneous record at intake-completion, with all
custody events accounted for. If an actual incident shows a custody
gap, the gap must be explicitly listed below with start/end
timestamps and known facts about who had access; silent smoothing-
over of gaps is the failure mode R6 catches.

WHAT THIS LOG IS AND IS NOT

  This log is the agency's own contemporaneous custody record, with
  the role/name redaction applied for public publication. It is not
  a court's finding that the custody chain was procedurally correct
  under applicable law, nor an IA's finding of compliance with
  agency policy. The log is the agency's claim; whether the claim
  satisfies any specific evidentiary standard, oversight process,
  or accreditation review is a matter for those processes.

  Any post-sealing modification of the custody log — adding a
  custody event after the fact, removing one, or changing the
  timestamp on one — produces a different SHA-256 over this fact
  body and is publicly detectable. The hash anchor freezes the
  agency's contemporaneous custody claim at the moment of seal.

This fact asserts only the custody-event timeline above, the
existence of zero non-case-personnel access events in the
intake-to-seal window, and the hashes of the separately-sealed
companion records, as the agency contemporaneously recorded them at
the moment of sealing. It does not assert:
  - that the chain-of-custody was procedurally correct under
    applicable law or agency policy,
  - that the role-to-individual binding is correct in the
    companion record (that is a separate evidentiary question),
  - that no custody events occurred that were not logged (the
    seal commits to the log as recorded; an undisclosed access
    by an authorized custodian who failed to log it would be a
    policy violation independent of the seal),
  - that the BWC-A3 mid-shift battery-swap was handled correctly
    under policy (compliance with policy is a separate IA-review
    question).
