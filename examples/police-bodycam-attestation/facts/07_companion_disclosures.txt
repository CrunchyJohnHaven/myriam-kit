REFUSAL RECORD — patterns the kit refused to seal

The kit does NOT seal the following types of facts, even if requested
by the agency, a co-equal authority, or any party with apparent
operational interest:

  R1. CONFIDENTIAL-INFORMANT IDENTIFYING INFORMATION
      No fact published here may name a confidential informant,
      include any handle that maps to a specific CI, expose a CI-
      provided detail that re-identifies them within the local
      context, or otherwise compromise CI safety. CI handling has
      its own access-controlled records under the agency's CI
      protocol; public seal is not a CI re-identification surface.

  R2. VICTIM-IDENTIFYING INFORMATION FOR PROTECTED CATEGORIES
      No fact may name (or expose any identifier reducible to) a
      sexual-assault victim, a minor, a victim of intimate-partner
      violence, a witness covered by victim-rights statutes, or any
      other protected-category individual whose identity is excluded
      by statute from public disclosure. State victim-rights
      statutes, federal statutes (VAWA, juvenile-record sealing
      analogs), and the agency's victim-rights protocol categorically
      exclude such data; the seal complies.

  R3. UNREDACTED BYSTANDER FACES OR OTHER UNINVOLVED-PARTY VISUAL
      IDENTIFIERS
      Per FACT 06, bystander faces (and uninvolved-vehicle license
      plates) are redacted from any segment in the public manifest
      where they were visible. The unredacted hashes are sealed for
      chain-of-custody integrity (so silent later redaction
      expansion is detectable), but the unredacted FILES are not
      published.

  R4. SINGLE-SIGNER SEALING WITHOUT IA CO-SIGNATURE
      Two-signer minimum: supervising sergeant of the involved
      unit AND a separate officer from internal affairs. Agencies
      whose IA function is under federal monitoring or court-
      appointed receivership are STRONGLY ENCOURAGED to add a
      third signer from the monitoring authority. The kit refuses
      to seal under a single signature; refusal is itself sealed.

  R5. PARTIAL-INCIDENT SEALING WITHOUT EXPLICIT GAP DISCLOSURE
      FACT 03 must enumerate ALL devices known to have been
      present and recording, including those whose footage is
      NOT in the seal — the latter as explicit gap entries with
      reasons. Silent omission of known-present devices is
      refused. (Applied in this example: BWC-A5 listed as gap E1.)

  R6. SEALING WITH UNEXPLAINED CHAIN-OF-CUSTODY ACCESS GAPS
      If FACT 05 shows a custody gap (a period during which the
      footage was accessible without logged custody, or during
      which the log itself has missing entries), the kit refuses
      to seal until the gap is explained in the log or explicitly
      flagged as a "custody-log anomaly." Sealing a smoothed-over
      gap is the failure mode this rule catches.

  R7. PERSONAL NAMES OF INVOLVED OFFICERS BEYOND ROLE-LEVEL
      Per FACT 02 / 05 / 08, the signing principals are named at
      role level; involved officers are identified by anonymized
      BWC ID (which the agency's separate role-to-individual
      binding maps to badge numbers and personal names under
      access control). The reason: officer-personal-name publication
      on Bitcoin creates a retaliation surface against the officers
      most likely to honestly attest to problematic numbers
      (whistleblowers, dissenters, members of historically-targeted
      demographic groups within the department). Agencies in
      jurisdictions where statute requires public personal-naming
      may amend their fork; the default refusal favors signer
      safety and whistleblower protection.

  R8. RE-CLASSIFICATION WITHOUT A SEALED AMENDMENT CHAIN
      The intake-time classification in FACT 02 is frozen. A later
      re-classification (e.g. "Type 2 — Non-lethal Use of Force"
      becomes "Type 1 — Lethal Use of Force" upon discovery that a
      previously-unrecognized severe injury occurred) requires a
      new seal that references this seal's Merkle root with the
      re-classification reason. The kit refuses to silently swap
      classifications.
