WHAT FAULT FLAGS MEAN AND DO NOT MEAN

  Each device's firmware self-reports a small set of operational
  fault flags during recording: clipped audio, dropped frames,
  occlusion warning (BWC-side; some firmware versions detect lens
  occlusion via brightness pattern), low-battery warning, storage-
  near-full warning, GPS-lock-lost (for devices with GPS metadata).
  These flags are sealed at intake. The flags are the device's own
  contemporaneous self-report; they do NOT certify the absence of
  faults the firmware did not detect (e.g. a firmware bug that
  produces silently corrupted video might not raise a fault flag),
  and they do NOT certify the presence of faults that did appear
  (the flags are accurate to the firmware's detection logic, which
  has known limits).

  The reason for sealing the fault flags AT INTAKE is to prevent
  the "the camera malfunctioned" post-hoc framing that arises only
  after public interest in the incident. If a device's contemporary
  fault log shows clean operation, a later "the camera had a known
  bug at that time" claim is publicly refutable against this record.
  If a device's contemporary log DOES show faults, those faults
  are anchored in the seal; the agency cannot later silently
  upgrade them or silently disappear them.

WHAT THIS FACT DOES NOT CERTIFY

  - That every device produced usable footage (a device can be on,
    recording, with no fault flags, and still produce occluded,
    low-light, or otherwise non-useful video; see "DOES NOT do"
    item 1 in the README).
  - That the device firmware was bug-free at the version listed
    (vendor firmware versions have had documented bugs; the
    sealed version-and-firmware-hash record allows later parties
    to cross-reference vendor advisories).
  - That the activation triggers were correctly configured for the
    incident type (activation policy is a separate FACT 02 BWC
    policy item; whether the policy was followed is a separate IA
    question).
  - That the officer-self-reported reason for the BWC-A5 exclusion
    (E1) is accurate — only that it is what was self-reported at
    intake and is anchored here so that any later contradiction
    is publicly visible.

This fact asserts only the per-device identification, firmware
version, recording window, activation trigger, battery state, and
self-reported fault flags as recorded at evidence-management intake.
It does not assert:
  - that the captured footage is a complete or representative
    record of the incident,
  - that the officer-self-reported reasons for any exclusion are
    accurate,
  - that the device-firmware self-report is exhaustive of all
    faults that occurred,
  - any conclusion about the conduct depicted in the footage.
