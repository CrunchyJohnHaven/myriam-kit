FACT 06 — REDACTION LOG (FICTIONAL)

This fact records every redaction event applied to the footage in
FACT 04 between evidence-management-system ingest (FACT 05 event
E12) and seal moment (FACT 05 event E17). Each redaction event
records: the source file, the type of redaction (face blur, audio
bleep, license-plate blur, etc.), the reason category, the
reviewer's role, and the SHA-256 hashes of BOTH the unredacted
source and the redacted output.

WHY THIS FACT IS LOAD-BEARING

Redaction is the most common vector for silent post-incident change
to body-camera footage. A department under pressure to release
footage may apply an initial redaction set, then under further
pressure quietly expand the redaction (blurring more faces, bleeping
more audio, blurring license plates that initially weren't blurred)
and re-release the "same" footage with a different content profile.
Sealing the redaction events AT INTAKE prevents this. Any post-seal
expansion of redaction:
  - changes the redacted-output hash (so the released file no
    longer matches what would have been released by the sealed
    redaction set), AND
  - if introduced as a new redaction event, requires either a new
    sealed amendment (referencing this seal's Merkle root) or a
    publicly-detectable departure from the sealed redaction
    profile.

The kit makes silent redaction expansion cryptographically visible.
Legitimate redaction expansion (e.g. a court order to redact
additional material discovered during litigation) is handled via
amendment seal; silent expansion is the failure mode.

REDACTION-METHOD TECHNICAL NOTE

  - Face blur applied via the DEMS's vendor-provided face-detection
    plus gaussian-blur pipeline at vendor-default strength; the
    blur is not reversible without the unredacted source.
  - License-plate blur applied via the same pipeline with vendor's
    plate-detection model; same non-reversibility.
  - Audio bleep applied via vendor tool overlaying a 1 kHz tone of
    duration equal to the bleeped segment; bleeped segment is not
    recoverable from the released file.

WHY UNREDACTED HASHES ARE LISTED HERE BUT THE UNREDACTED FILES ARE
NOT PUBLISHED

The unredacted hashes are listed for two reasons:
  1. Forensic integrity. A future agency claim that "we applied no
     redactions to this segment" or "we applied a different
     redaction pass" is publicly refutable: the unredacted hash
     and the redacted hash are both committed; the gap between
     them is exactly the redaction event listed above.
  2. Chain-of-custody continuity. The unredacted source remains in
     the agency's DEMS under retention policy. Any lawful release
     of the unredacted source (e.g. under court-ordered discovery
     to defense counsel under a protective order) can be re-hashed
     against the unredacted hash above to confirm integrity of the
     unredacted source.

The unredacted FILES themselves are NOT in this public manifest per
refusal rule R3 (bystander faces) and R1/R2 (protected-category
identifiers). The HASH being public does not re-identify any
bystander or protected-category individual; the hash is opaque to
the content. A hash alone of a video file containing a bystander's
face is not a re-identification vector.

NO REDACTIONS APPLIED TO METADATA SIDECARS

The metadata sidecar files (FACT 04 rows 13-22) are byte-identical
between the agency's internal copy and the publicly-hashed copy.
Activation logs, GPS tracks, accelerometer data, and the DEMS
ingest manifest do not contain bystander or protected-category
content and are not redacted. Their public hashes match their
internal hashes.

POST-SEAL REDACTION POLICY

After this seal moment, any additional redaction (in response to
victim-rights statute compliance, court order, settlement
agreement, or any other lawful trigger) requires either:
  - publication of an AMENDMENT SEAL that references this seal's
    Merkle root in FACT 02 and lists the new redaction events in
    FACT 06 with the trigger reason, OR
  - documentation in the agency's DEMS audit trail in a manner
    that allows oversight authorities to compare the released
    file against the sealed redacted hash and verify the
    additional redaction was lawful.

Silent post-seal redaction expansion that does not produce an
amendment seal is publicly detectable (the released file's hash
will not match the sealed redacted hash) and is the exact failure
mode this fact prevents.

This fact asserts only the redaction events listed above, the
reviewer-role identification, the unredacted and redacted SHA-256
hashes of the affected files, and the no-redactions-applied-to-
metadata-sidecars statement, as recorded at intake-completion. It
does not assert:
  - that the redaction reasons are exhaustive of all redactions
    that might be legally required under any specific jurisdiction's
    public-records, victim-rights, or court-ordered redaction
    regime,
  - that the redactions applied were sufficient to comply with all
    such regimes (a court or oversight authority may later order
    additional redactions, which would trigger an amendment seal),
  - that the vendor face-detection and plate-detection models are
    100% accurate (some bystander faces or plates may not have
    been detected; legitimate post-seal redaction of missed items
    follows the amendment-seal protocol above).
