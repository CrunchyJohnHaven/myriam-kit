FACT 01 — EXAMPLE NOTICE

This publication is an EXAMPLE demonstration of how a U.S. law-
enforcement agency — municipal police department, county sheriff's
office, state police, transit police, or other agency operating body-
worn cameras (BWCs), in-car dashboard cameras (dashcams), and
interview-room recording systems — can use myriam-kit to seal a
chain-of-custody anchor on body-camera footage at the moment of
evidence-management-system ingest completion. The incident identifier,
agency identification, ORI, device serial numbers, firmware versions,
file hashes, custody-event timeline, redaction events, and all other
particulars in subsequent facts are ALL FICTIONAL.

The purpose of this publication is to show the SHAPE of a real body-
camera footage seal. A real attestation would replace each fact body
with the actual artifacts of the actual incident's recordings.

This kit is intended to compose with — not replace — the existing
chain-of-custody, evidence-management, public-records, victim-rights,
and discovery procedures of the jurisdiction running the case (state
public-records laws, federal civil-rights statutes, DOJ pattern-or-
practice oversight where applicable, agency BWC policy, prosecutorial
Brady/Giglio obligations, etc.). The MYRIAM seal provides Bitcoin-
anchored tamper-evidence on top of whatever chain-of-custody record
the agency's digital-evidence-management system already maintains. It
does NOT itself satisfy any court's admissibility test, compel public
release of footage, or substitute for any oversight process;
admissibility, release, and oversight are legal and policy questions,
not cryptographic ones.

This kit uses a TWO-SIGNER MINIMUM (supervising sergeant + internal-
affairs officer) for sealing, mirroring the hospital readmission kit's
Chief Medical Officer + Quality Officer pattern. Two-signer is non-
negotiable in this kit; the gaming vectors that body-camera footage
chain-of-custody historically faces are exactly the kind that single-
signer agency fraud has concealed in past cases.

This fact asserts only that the publication is an example, not a real
body-camera footage record. No real incident is hereby investigated.
No real officer, suspect, complainant, victim, witness, bystander,
informant, or third party is hereby identified.
