FACT 01 — EXAMPLE NOTICE

This publication is an EXAMPLE demonstration of how a U.S. health
insurer (medical director, utilization-review physician, UM program),
a provider organization (hospital UM team, large practice, IPA), a
state insurance regulator, or a patient advocacy organization can use
myriam-kit to seal a prior-authorization decision attestation at the
moment a coverage-of-services decision is rendered, so that
post-facto revision of the prior-auth record — by the carrier
(silent criteria revision, suppression of favorable evidence,
appeal-window manipulation, post-facto denial of approved
authorizations), by the provider (silent revision of what was
submitted), or by either side as to "what was decided when" —
becomes cryptographically detectable. The payer, carrier NAIC code,
authorization number, provider organization NPI, member identifier
handle, clinical guideline citation, evidence hash list, decision,
appeal window, signing physician and license number, and companion
disclosures in subsequent facts are ALL FICTIONAL.

The purpose: show the SHAPE of a real prior-authorization decision
seal. A real attestation would replace each fact body with the
actual artifacts of the actual decision at the moment of decision.

This kit is intended to compose with — not replace — the existing
prior-authorization, X12 278 transaction, ERISA §503 internal-claims
procedure, state utilization-review statute, state external-review
(IRO) program, CMS Interoperability and Prior Authorization Final
Rule (CMS-0057-F), NCQA UM accreditation, and provider-agreement
contractual framework of the carrier and the jurisdictions in which
the decision is being rendered. The MYRIAM seal provides
Bitcoin-anchored tamper-evidence on top of whatever prior-auth file,
EDI transaction record, FHIR API response, or UM-system record the
carrier already maintains. It does NOT itself decide coverage,
satisfy any state's UR statute, satisfy ERISA §503 procedural
requirements, substitute for internal appeal, substitute for
state-mandated external review, or substitute for the carrier's
regulatory filings.

This fact asserts only that the publication is an example, not a
real prior-authorization decision. No real authorization is hereby
rendered. No real patient, provider, prescriber, or licensed
clinical reviewer is hereby identified.
