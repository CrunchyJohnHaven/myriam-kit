FACT 06 — APPEAL WINDOW (FICTIONAL)

The appeal window for the decision in FACT 05 is set at the
moment of decision and sealed here so that subsequent silent
shortening of the window — by re-narrating the "notice date,"
by changing the expedited-review eligibility, by changing the
external-review pathway, or by missing the carrier's own
deadlines without disclosure — is publicly refutable. Per
refusal rule R7, no decision may be sealed without the appeal
window being calculated and disclosed at the moment of
decision.

  WINDOW NON-MODIFICATION COMMITMENT

The carrier attests, by sealing this fact, that:

  - The deadlines above are the deadlines as of the date of
    decision and cannot be silently shortened later. A
    subsequent assertion by the carrier that a different
    "notice date" applies (e.g. the date the notice was
    received by the member, computed from a later USPS
    delivery date, vs. the date of generation) is publicly
    refutable against the notice date stated above.
  - For expedited-review-eligible cases (where this fact says
    "yes" above), the expedited pathway is committed and
    cannot be silently downgraded to standard. A subsequent
    carrier assertion of "we determined the case was not
    urgent" is itself an adverse determination subject to
    appeal.
  - For external-review path-selection: if the carrier asserts
    a different IRO path on a subsequent communication than
    the one sealed above, the change is itself an adverse
    procedural determination subject to challenge.

This fact asserts only the appeal-window deadlines, the
expedited-review eligibility, the external-review path
selection, and the window non-modification commitment for the
fictional decision. It does NOT assert:
  - that the deadlines are statutorily correct (a separate
    legal question against the controlling state and federal
    statutes and the plan document),
  - that the member or provider will succeed on appeal,
  - that the IRO will affirm or reverse the carrier's
    determination,
  - that the carrier's UM program description on file with
    the state insurance department is internally consistent
    with the deadlines stated above (any inconsistency is
    itself a regulator-reportable issue).
