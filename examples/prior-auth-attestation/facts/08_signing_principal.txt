  RATIONALE FOR INDIVIDUAL-LEVEL (NOT ROLE-LEVEL) PUBLICATION

    Per refusal rules R3 and R4, the signing principal of a
    prior-authorization decision must be a credentialed
    licensed clinician (default: a licensed physician), and
    the seal records the individual signer's name, license
    number, and licensure state. Unlike the hospital-readmission
    attestation kit (which uses role-level publication for the
    CMO and Quality VP because per-physician readmission rates
    create retaliation surfaces against clinicians who advocate
    for sicker patients), the prior-auth kit uses
    individual-level publication for the signing physician
    because:

      - The licensed signer is the legally accountable
        decisionmaker for the determination,
      - Per-signer decisioning volume (decisions per day,
        median time per decision, override rate against AI
        recommendation) is the load-bearing metric for
        detecting the AI-rubber-stamp failure mode that R4
        prevents and that is the subject of major recent
        litigation against carriers (see README "Historical
        context"),
      - The signing physician's individual NPI, state
        medical license, and board certification are already
        public information via NPPES and state medical board
        registries — the seal aggregates them with the
        decision content but does not newly expose private
        information.

    Where the signing principal is a non-physician licensed
    clinical reviewer under a carrier UR policy that the state
    UR statute permits (e.g. RN reviewers for initial-screen
    determinations in some states), the same individual-level
    publication standard applies — the signer's professional
    license type, license number, and state are published.

    Per refusal rule R3, the kit refuses to seal a decision
    where the named signer is not a credentialed clinical
    reviewer (i.e. clerical staff, intake personnel, or an AI
    system named as the signer).

  THE SIGNING PRINCIPAL SHOULD NOT INCLUDE IN THIS FACT:

    - Personal phone numbers, home addresses, or personal
      email of the signing physician
    - Family member names or other personal context
    - Trade secrets about the carrier's UM decisionmaking
      systems beyond what FACT 07 discloses for the
      AI-system transparency requirement
    - Detailed compensation or productivity-quota terms of
      the signing physician's employment with the carrier
      (the existence of productivity quotas and their
      relationship to override authority is disclosable in
      FACT 07 per R4 attestation; the specific dollar
      compensation is not)

This fact asserts only the signing-principal identification,
licensure, role, substantive-review attestation, and the
rationale for individual-level publication, for the fictional
decision. It does NOT assert any of those fields about any
real medical director, licensed clinical reviewer, carrier
employee, or carrier organization. It does not assert that
the signing principal is free of all institutional
decisionmaking pressure (refusal rule R8 in the README
addresses disclosure of coercion-amplification context where
present); the substantive-review attestation is the signer's
representation, not an independent verification.
