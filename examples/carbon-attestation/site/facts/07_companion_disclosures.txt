COMPANION DISCLOSURES (sealed alongside FACT 03 so the quantification
cannot be read in isolation)

  C1. PENDING METHODOLOGY DISPUTES
      Methodology FCS-METH-REDD-001 v3.2 (FACT 02) is subject to
      ongoing academic critique regarding reference-region selection
      bias in REDD+ baselines (see e.g. West et al. 2020 Science,
      Coffield et al. 2022, Guizar-Coutiño et al. 2022). The
      project's chosen reference-region selection is internally
      consistent with the methodology but not independently
      adjudicated. (FICTIONAL framing of a real ongoing debate.)

  C2. NON-PERMANENCE RISK
      The buffer-pool contribution (FACT 03, 20% per VCS AFOLU
      Buffer Tool) is the methodology's defense against reversal.
      Recent literature (Badgley et al. 2022 GCB on California
      forest offsets; Patterson et al. 2022 PNAS) shows that buffer
      pools have been substantively depleted by real reversal events,
      including wildfires. The 20% buffer reserved here may be
      insufficient against future climate-driven reversal risk in
      this project area. The project SHOULD NOT be cited as
      "permanent" without an additional century-scale reversal
      mechanism.

  C3. LEAKAGE
      The 10% leakage deduction (FACT 03) is methodology-default and
      does not reflect activity-specific leakage measurement. Real
      project-specific leakage to neighboring forest areas is not
      directly measured for this project type. The deduction is a
      regulatory placeholder, not a measurement.

  C4. ADDITIONALITY
      The project's additionality argument (the claim that avoided
      emissions would not have occurred without carbon finance) is
      methodology-compliant but rests on counterfactual modeling
      that no methodology can verify directly. The project asserts
      additionality; the methodology accepts the assertion under
      its rules; neither constitutes proof.

REFUSAL RECORD — patterns the kit refused to seal

The kit does NOT seal the following fact patterns, even if requested by
the project developer, verifier, or registry:

  R1. Claims of "additionality" stated as fact rather than as a
      methodology-compliant counterfactual assertion (additionality
      cannot be empirically verified for the project alone, and
      sealing it as fact creates a misleading record)
  R2. Claims of "permanence" without explicit time-horizon and
      buffer-pool / insurance mechanism disclosure
  R3. Claims of "no leakage" without project-specific measurement
      (methodology-default leakage deductions are NOT measurement)
  R4. Quantifications generated before the verifier's site visit
      and document review are complete
  R5. Issuance records for credits also issued or claimed on a
      different registry without explicit cross-registry disclosure
  R6. Retirement records that fail to name the retirement
      beneficiary (anonymous retirement undermines the public-good
      function of retirement)
  R7. FPIC claims unsupported by attestable documentation hashes
  R8. Article 6.2 / 6.4 issuance without a host-country letter of
      authorization (LoA) hash sealed in FACT 06
  R9. Sealing at request of any party other than the project
      developer + verifier jointly for that project
  R10. Co-benefits claims sealed as "certified" when only
       "self-asserted + verifier-reviewed"

If during attestation any request matching R1–R10 was made and
refused, that refusal would be documented as a sealed fact:
  "Refusal #N: party X requested sealing of [pattern]; the kit
  refused per refusal-rule R[m]."
