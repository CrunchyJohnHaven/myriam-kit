FACT 08 — SIGNING PRINCIPAL (FICTIONAL)

A carbon attestation requires THREE distinct signing principals (the
project developer, the third-party verifier, and the registry of
record) for the seal to carry its full forensic weight. The kit accepts
fewer signers, but each missing signer is itself a sealed disclosure
and weakens the attestation's coverage in a known way.

  Organization:           Fictional Forest Conservation NGO (FICTIONAL)
  Jurisdiction of
    incorporation:        FICTIONAL_COUNTRY (e.g. registered NGO)
  Project role:           Developer + monitoring entity
  Signing officer:        [Fictional Name], Executive Director
  Cryptographic key:      (a real attestation would include a PGP
                          public key fingerprint here; omitted in this
                          example because no real person exists)
  Attestation timestamp:  2026-01-15T10:30:00Z (FICTIONAL)
  Signed scope:           This signatory attests to the truth of the

                          hashes in FACT 02, FACT 04, and FACT 07

In a real attestation, the signing principals section should include:

  - Each signatory's legal name + role + organization
  - Each signatory's cryptographic key fingerprint (PGP or similar)
  - The scope each signatory is willing to attest to (signers should
    not be presumed to attest to facts outside their epistemic reach;
    e.g. the verifier does not attest to FPIC obtained by the
    developer years before the verifier's engagement)
  - Disclosure of any prior commercial relationship between developer
    and verifier within a 5-year lookback (also in FACT 07 C7)

The signing principal section SHOULD NOT include:

  - Personal contact information for individual officers
  - Home addresses or other locating information
  - Identifying information about complainants or grievance-process
    participants (those go in FACT 07 only as aggregate hashes)
  - Identifying information about indigenous community members
    individually (only the community / authority that gave FPIC, as
    such authority is empowered to act in public capacity)

If any of the three required signing principals is missing or refuses
to sign, the kit can still produce a seal — but the seal must
explicitly note the missing signer as a companion disclosure. A
"developer-only" seal of issuance, for example, is forensically much
weaker than a developer + verifier + registry seal, and the kit
refuses to let that distinction be hidden.

This fact asserts only the signing principal identification fields for
the fictional attestation as declared. It does not assert any of
those fields about any real project developer, verifier, registry, or
officer, nor that the signing principals' attested scope is
itself sufficient to make the project's carbon claims true.
