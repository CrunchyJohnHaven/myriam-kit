This page is a sealed publication of 8 facts under the MYRIAM-kit protocol. Each fact below has been hashed (SHA-256), combined into a Bitcoin-style Merkle root, and committed to the Bitcoin blockchain via OpenTimestamps against two independent calendar operators. Any change to any fact would produce a Merkle root different from the one committed to Bitcoin, and would therefore be detectable by any member of the public.

What this seal does and does not assert: the seal records what was attested in its exact byte form at the sealed time. It does NOT assert that the credited activity is additional, that sequestered carbon will remain sequestered, that emissions are not displaced via leakage, that FPIC was genuine, that the verifier is in fact independent, or that the methodology is scientifically valid. See the kit README for the full list of what is and is not covered.

How to Verify

  1. Download all 8 fact files from facts/.
  2. Recompute each file's SHA-256 and confirm it matches the per-fact hash shown above.
  3. Compute the Merkle root (algorithm in SPEC §3), or run verify.py.
  4. Verify the OpenTimestamps receipt against Bitcoin: ots verify merkle_root.txt.ots (with both merkle_root.txt and merkle_root.txt.ots present in the same directory).
  5. Independent IPFS check: ipfs add -r --cid-version=1 -Q site-dir/ and confirm the resulting CID matches {{IPFS_CID}}.
  6. Diff the registry's current project page (FACT 02 registry of record) against the bytes sealed here. Any discrepancy is either a registry update post-attestation (which should itself be a new sealed publication referencing this one) or a silent registry modification (which is the failure mode this kit detects).

What this protocol promises (and doesn't)

The protocol delivers detectability of tampering with the byte-level record of what was attested, not impossibility of tampering. It delivers content-addressed mirroring at the IPFS layer, but not guaranteed long-term pinning durability without additional pin redundancy. It does not assert that any sealed fact is true; it asserts only that the fact was published in its current bytewise form at the sealed time.

For carbon attestations specifically, the protocol does NOT verify additionality, permanence, leakage, co-benefits, FPIC sufficiency, verifier independence, methodology validity, registry correctness, or buyer-claim correctness. Those are the harder problems and the seal cannot solve them.

See the project's threat model for the full list of what the protocol does and does not defend against.
