WHY THIS SECTION NAMES NATURAL PERSONS RATHER THAN ROLES ONLY

The court-evidence-custody kit's default is role-level publication
because forensic examiners face documented post-hoc harassment risks
when their personal names are tied to specific cases on a permanent
public ledger. The insurance-claim-attestation kit's default goes the
OTHER way — natural-person publication of the licensed adjuster of
record — for three reasons:

  1. State adjuster licensing is already public. A natural person
     who chooses to practice as a licensed adjuster has accepted that
     their name appears on state-DOI public licensee rolls; the seal
     is not creating new public-name exposure.
  2. The adjuster's license is the load-bearing accountability layer
     for the decision. A coverage decision made by an unlicensed
     person, or by a person whose license does not cover the line,
     is itself a coverage-integrity flag; the public seal must be
     able to surface that.
  3. Insurance bad-faith and unfair-claims-handling statutes
     contemplate per-claim adjuster accountability; the public seal
     must support, not undermine, that accountability regime.

In jurisdictions where adjuster personal-naming on a public claim
seal is itself prohibited or restricted, the kit may be amended to
publish role-only at this layer, with the adjuster's name held in the
carrier's claim file under the regulator's existing access controls.
That is a kit fork, not the default.

The signing principal section SHOULD NOT include:

  - Personal contact information (home address, personal phone) of
    adjuster or supervisors.
  - Information about adjusters' family members.
  - The insured's, claimant's, or any private third party's legal
    name (see refusal-rules R1 and R3).
  - Trade secrets about the carrier's reserves, IBNR calculations,
    or pricing methodologies (these belong in confidential
    regulatory filings, not public seals).

This fact asserts only the signing principal identification fields
for the fictional claim decision as declared. It does not assert any
of those fields about any real carrier, any real adjuster, any real
supervisor, or any real claim.
