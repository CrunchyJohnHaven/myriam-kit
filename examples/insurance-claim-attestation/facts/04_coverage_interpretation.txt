FACT 04 — COVERAGE INTERPRETATION AT MOMENT OF DECISION (FICTIONAL)

This fact freezes, at the moment of decision, the carrier's reading
of the policy provisions that govern the claim in FACT 02. Any post-
decision claim that "the policy meant something else" or "we always
interpreted exclusion X to apply" is publicly refutable against this
record. A future appellate court, a state insurance department
examiner, or the insured's coverage counsel may diff the carrier's
later-stated interpretation against this sealed interpretation.

The full coverage memo (item 11 in FACT 03) is hashed in FACT 03; the
material interpretive points are reproduced in summary form here so
that the seal records WHAT the carrier read the contract to mean, not
just that a memo exists.

COVERAGE PROVISIONS AT ISSUE

  1. COLLISION COVERAGE (Part D of base form)
     Interpretation at decision: the loss arises from a covered
     collision with a non-fixed object; first-party physical
     damage to the named insured's vehicle is within the grant of
     coverage; deductible of $1,000 applies per the dec page.

  2. BODILY INJURY LIABILITY (Part A of base form)
     Interpretation at decision: the loss is alleged to involve
     bodily injury to Claimant-B (a non-insured); coverage is
     available subject to the per-person limit of $100,000 and
     per-accident limit of $300,000 stated on the dec page.

  3. MEDICAL PAYMENTS (Endorsement END-MEDPAY-5K)
     Interpretation at decision: Insured-X reported no first-
     party injury at FNOL or in the recorded statement (item 2,
     FACT 03); no MedPay payment is requested or anticipated; the
     coverage remains available should a first-party injury claim
     be made within the policy's medical-payments time period.

  4. EXCLUSIONS REVIEWED AND FOUND NOT TO APPLY
     The following commonly-litigated exclusions were considered
     and were determined NOT to apply on the facts known at the
     date of decision:
       - Intentional acts exclusion: no evidence of intentional
         conduct (police report does not allege intent; no
         criminal charges filed against insured).
       - Business use exclusion: vehicle was in personal use at
         time of loss per insured's recorded statement.
       - Racing / speed contest exclusion: not alleged by any
         party.
       - Material misrepresentation in application: application
         file reviewed; no misrepresentation identified.
       - Late notice / prejudice: claim was reported within 90
         minutes of the loss; no late-notice prejudice argument.

  5. EXCLUSIONS NOT YET FULLY ANALYZED (see FACT 06 disclosures)
     None at the date of this decision. If a later-discovered fact
     pattern triggers an exclusion analysis (e.g., subsequent
     discovery that the vehicle was being used for ride-share work
     at time of loss), that analysis would proceed under the
     carrier's standard reformation / reservation-of-rights
     procedure, with the original decision sealed here remaining as
     the basis on which payment was approved.

GOVERNING LAW

  The policy is governed by the law of [Fictional state]. The
  carrier's interpretation of the policy is offered as the
  carrier's reading of the contract; final interpretation, in the
  event of dispute, rests with a court of competent jurisdiction
  applying [Fictional state] insurance law.

  In particular, this fact does NOT assert that the carrier's
  reading would prevail in a coverage dispute. It records what the
  carrier's reading WAS at the moment the payment decision was
  rendered, so that a later-revised reading is publicly detectable.

This fact asserts only the policy provisions the carrier identified as
governing, the endorsement set in force, and the carrier's
contemporaneous interpretation of those provisions at the moment of
decision. It does not assert:
  - that the carrier's interpretation is legally correct,
  - that no other exclusion could be raised in a coverage dispute,
  - that the policy was in force at the date of loss (premium-and-
    cancellation analysis is its own separate review),
  - that the underlying facts of the loss are as the parties have
    described them.
