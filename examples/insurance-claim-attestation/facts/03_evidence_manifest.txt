Each hash was computed with SHA-256 over the exact bytes of the file
as it existed in the carrier's claims-management system at the moment
the coverage decision was rendered (date in FACT 02), BEFORE any post-
decision modification.

Any party — insured, claimant counsel, the carrier's own future
audit, a state insurance department investigator, an appellate court,
a reinsurer reviewing the file — receiving any of the above items
through normal claim-file production (insured's request under state
claim-file-access statutes, discovery, examination under oath, etc.)
may re-hash the file they receive with SHA-256 and compare to the row
above. If the hashes match, the recipient holds the exact bytes that
were sealed at the moment of decision. If the hashes do not match, the
file has been altered (intentionally or accidentally) since the
decision; the recipient should raise this immediately with the
carrier, opposing counsel, and the state insurance department.

In this fictional example, the hashes above are deterministic
placeholders. A real claim would replace these with hashes of the
actual evidence files.

What is INTENTIONALLY ABSENT from this manifest:

  - Claimant SSN, full DOB, full home address, or phone number.
    The carrier knows this; the public seal does not. Re-
    identification of a private claimant via the public seal is
    NOT a feature of the kit.
  - Diagnostic codes that would identify a rare or stigmatizing
    condition. The third-party medical bill set (item 8) records
    CPT/HCPCS billing codes and billed amounts only; ICD-10
    diagnoses, treatment narratives, mental-health detail, HIV
    status, substance-use treatment records, etc. are NOT in the
    public manifest, even by hash, where their hash plus context
    could be reversed by an interested party. See refusal-rule R2.
  - Witness legal names. Witnesses are referred to by handle
    (W-1, W-2, ...) — see refusal-rule R3.
  - Surveillance photographs of the claimant taken outside the
    moment of the loss (SIU surveillance of a suspected fraudulent
    claimant is its own evidentiary regime and does not belong on
    the public seal of a coverage decision).

This fact asserts only the manifest of evidence-file-level hashes,
sizes, sources, and submission timing above, and the integrity
property the hashes provide. It does not assert:
  - that the evidence is authentic,
  - that the evidence is sufficient,
  - that any party's account of events is correct,
  - that the third-party claimant is or is not legally entitled to
    recover (covered separately in FACTS 04 and 05).
