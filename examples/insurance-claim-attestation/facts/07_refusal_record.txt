FACT 07 — REFUSAL RECORD (FICTIONAL)

The kit does NOT seal the following types of facts, even if requested
by the carrier, by claimant counsel, by a third-party claimant, or by
a co-equal authority. A refusal itself may be sealed as a fact
("This party requested sealing of X; the kit refused per refusal-rule
Y"), creating a public record of attempted misuse.

R1. CLAIMANT SSN, FULL DOB, FULL ADDRESS, OR PHONE NUMBER
    The carrier knows these from the policy file; the public seal
    does not need them and must not carry them. The claim number in
    FACT 02 is the appropriate handle for parties with standing to
    locate the claim file. The public seal is NOT a re-identification
    index of insureds or claimants.
    Identity verification of the insured for purposes of the
    coverage analysis is performed against the carrier's policy file
    using the policy-file binding (carrier-internal), NOT against
    publicly-published claimant identifiers.

R2. DIAGNOSIS NARRATIVES, TREATMENT SPECIFICS, OR PROCEDURE CODES
    THAT COULD IDENTIFY RARE OR STIGMATIZING CONDITIONS
    Even by hash. Even by file-level fingerprint. The reason: a
    medical bill set's hash plus the case context (third-party
    bodily injury, specific date and location of loss) can be
    reversed by an interested party — opposing counsel, an
    employer, an insurer running a separate underwriting decision —
    to re-identify the claimant's diagnosis. The public seal
    records CPT/HCPCS billing-code summaries and billed amounts
    only (item 8, FACT 03); ICD-10 diagnoses, treatment narratives,
    mental-health detail, HIV status, substance-use treatment
    records, reproductive-health records, and rare-disease
    indicators are NEVER in the public seal, even by hash.
    Medical detail is appropriately discoverable through normal
    civil-litigation procedures with protective orders, NOT through
    a permanent public ledger.

R3. WITNESS NAMES (EXCEPT WHERE PUBLIC RECORD)
    Witnesses are referred to by handle (W-1, W-2, ...) in FACT 03.
    The carrier's claim file binds handles to specific witness
    individuals; that binding is discoverable in litigation under
    normal civil procedure. The public seal does NOT publish
    witness legal names because witnesses are often unwilling
    participants in another party's dispute and public-ledger
    publication exposes them to harassment, retaliation, or
    discovery they did not consent to.
    EXCEPTION: where a witness is a public-record actor (a
    responding police officer named in the police report, item 4 of
    FACT 03; a public-agency representative; a treating physician
    only where the physician's identity is already public via a
    state medical-board complaint or published court ruling), the
    handle may be cross-referenced to a public-record citation
    rather than masked. This exception is narrowly construed.

R4. SEALING A PARTIAL-INVESTIGATION DECISION PRESENTED AS FINAL
    The kit refuses to seal a "decision" that is in fact a
    pre-investigation pre-decision, an interim payment, a coverage
    reservation, or any other non-final action presented as if it
    were a coverage decision. The decision date in FACT 02 must
    correspond to the actual moment of final coverage
    determination; interim actions are sealed (if at all) as
    interim actions with explicit non-final labeling.
    Rationale: sealing a pre-investigation rejection as if it were
    a final decision provides false-finality cover for an adjuster
    who has not yet done the investigation; sealing a token
    interim payment as if it were the final payment provides cover
    for later silent denial of the balance. The decision date and
    the disposition status (FACT 02 + FACT 05) must accurately
    label whether this is the final coverage decision or an interim
    action.

R5. SEALING BY ANYONE OTHER THAN THE LICENSED ADJUSTER OF RECORD
    The seal records the carrier's coverage decision. Only the
    licensed adjuster of record (or, where the adjuster is acting
    under a managing general agent / TPA delegation, the
    appropriately licensed signing principal of that delegated
    authority) may sign the seal under the carrier's name. The
    insured, claimant counsel, a state insurance department
    examiner, a reinsurer, or a third-party reviewer may use the
    SAME kit to seal THEIR OWN independent analysis of the same
    claim (e.g., "the insured publishes this hash of the evidence
    they submitted; the carrier's seal must include this hash, or
    the carrier is missing evidence"), but they may not seal the
    CARRIER'S decision under the carrier's name. The signing
    principal in FACT 08 is the only valid signer for the
    carrier-side decision seal.
    Independent counter-seals by the insured or claimant are a
    feature: they make silent evidence-omission detectable.

R6. CLAIMS OF LEGAL FINALITY OR PRECLUSION
    The kit will not seal a fact body that claims the decision is
    "binding," "final and non-appealable," "preclusive," or any
    other legal conclusion. The decision is the carrier's
    decision; the insured retains all rights to demand appraisal
    (where the policy provides), to invoke the policy's appeal
    procedure, to file a complaint with the state insurance
    department, and to litigate. Legal finality is a court
    function; the kit anchors the carrier's contemporaneous
    decision, not a legal conclusion about its enforceability.

R7. RETROACTIVE COVERAGE INTERPRETATION CHANGES IN A
    PURPORTED "AMENDMENT" SEAL
    If after sealing the carrier wishes to revise the coverage
    interpretation in FACT 04 or the payment amounts in FACT 05,
    the revision MUST be sealed as a NEW publication referencing
    this seal by Merkle root. The kit refuses to re-seal a modified
    version of this seal under the same identifiers. The original
    sealed decision is permanent; revisions are new publications,
    transparently linked. This prevents a carrier from claiming
    "the seal always said X" when X was substituted later.

R8. SEALING UNDER PRESSURE TO DENY A CLAIM THAT IS NOT YET FULLY
    INVESTIGATED
    See R4. The kit also refuses, as a matter of design, to seal
    a denial whose stated reason code is "investigation incomplete
    but denying for procedural reasons." A denial seal must rest on
    substantive reason codes tied to specific policy provisions
    (FACT 04) and specific evidence (FACT 03). A denial that
    cannot be tied to a specific provision and specific evidence
    indicates the decision is not yet ripe to be sealed.

R9. SEALING THE EXISTENCE OF A NON-COMPETE OR NON-DISCLOSURE BAR
    ON THE CLAIMANT
    No fact body sealed under this kit may include language
    obligating the claimant to non-disclosure of the decision
    terms, the existence of the claim, or the seal itself.
    Coverage decisions made under threat of post-settlement
    non-disclosure are an established bad-faith pattern; the kit
    refuses to be the vehicle for that pattern. (The carrier may
    of course include a non-disclosure clause in a separate
    settlement agreement under applicable law; the kit just won't
    seal the existence of the bar inside the public publication.)

R10. SEALING WHILE BAD-FAITH LITIGATION AGAINST THE CARRIER IS
     PENDING ON THIS CLAIM WITHOUT EXPRESS COURT CONCURRENCE
    If at decision time the carrier is already in bad-faith
    litigation regarding this same claim, sealing without express
    court concurrence is refused. The seal would be entering a
    contested record on the carrier's terms; that is a court
    function. Where the court directs sealing as part of a
    settlement order, the kit may be used; where not, the kit
    refuses.

REFUSALS THIS CLAIM: NONE. (FICTIONAL)

If during claim handling any request matching R1-R10 was made and
refused, that refusal would be documented as a sealed fact:
  "Refusal #N: party X requested sealing of [pattern]; the kit
  refused per refusal-rule R[m]."

This fact asserts only the refusal-rule list and the refusal record
at the moment of decision. It does not assert that the refusal-rule
list is exhaustive of all PII or bad-faith patterns, or that the
absence of recorded refusals means no improper sealing pressure was
applied (only that no formal refusal was triggered).
