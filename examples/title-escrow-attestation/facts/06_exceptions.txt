FACT 06 — EXCEPTIONS (FICTIONAL)

This fact records the items the title officer could NOT verify
from the search, and the items that fall outside the search's
coverage. The exceptions list is load-bearing: a buyer or
lender reading only the chain (FACT 03) and the encumbrances
(FACT 04) without reading the exceptions risks treating the
search as more complete than it is. The exceptions here
parallel — but do NOT replace — the standard exceptions in an
ALTA commitment; the underwriter's commitment is the
contractual document that determines what is INSURED versus
EXCEPTED.

STANDARD EXCEPTIONS (matters customarily excepted from any
title commitment)

  X1. SURVEY MATTERS
      Any encroachment, overlap, boundary-line dispute, party-
      wall agreement, shortage in area, or other matter that
      would be disclosed by an accurate survey or physical
      inspection of the parcel and that is NOT shown by the
      public records. This search did not include a survey;
      the FACT 02 legal description is the recorded
      description, which may differ from the occupied
      boundary.

  X2. UNRECORDED RIGHTS OF PERSONS IN POSSESSION
      Any right, claim, or interest of any party in
      possession of the parcel that is NOT shown by the
      public records. Tenancy claims, sub-tenancy claims,
      adverse-possession claims, prescriptive-easement
      claims, and similar matters that depend on facts
      outside the recorder's index are not addressed by
      this search.

  X3. UNRECORDED EASEMENTS, INCLUDING EASEMENTS BY IMPLICATION,
      NECESSITY, OR PRESCRIPTION
      Easements established by long use, by necessity at
      severance of common ownership, or by other non-recording
      means do not appear in the recorder's index and are
      outside the scope of this search.

  X4. MECHANIC'S LIENS THAT MAY ATTACH BUT ARE NOT YET
      RECORDED
      Under the law of the situs state, a contractor who has
      furnished labor or materials in the recent past may
      have a mechanic's-lien right that has not yet been
      perfected by recording. The search effective date
      shows what was RECORDED; any unperfected-but-still-
      timely lien right is outside the scope.

  X5. WATER RIGHTS, RIPARIAN RIGHTS, AND PRIOR-APPROPRIATION
      CLAIMS
      Water rights in the situs state are governed by a
      separate regime (riparian, prior-appropriation, or
      hybrid depending on the state and the type of water).
      This search does not address water rights; a separate
      water-rights search is required if water rights are
      material to the transaction.

  X6. MINERAL, OIL, AND GAS RIGHTS SEVERED BEFORE THE ROOT
      OF TITLE
      The chain in FACT 03 begins at 1923. Any mineral,
      oil, or gas severance occurring before that date is
      outside the scope of this search; a separate pre-
      root mineral-search is required if subsurface rights
      are material.

SEARCH-SPECIFIC EXCEPTIONS

  X7. PRE-1923 PREDECESSOR CHAIN
      The Fictional Land Co. predecessor chain (any
      conveyances prior to the 1923 root of title in FACT 03
      Idx 1) was NOT searched. Marketable-record-title-act
      statutes in some states cut off claims based on pre-
      root recordings after a statutory period; whether
      such cutoff applies in the situs state is a legal
      determination not made here.

  X8. FAMILY-COURT-SEALED MATTERS
      The family-court search in FACT 05 was limited to
      publicly accessible docket entries. Any sealed
      family-court matter affecting the property (sealed
      domestic-violence protection orders, sealed
      paternity actions, sealed mediation outcomes) is
      not visible to the officer and is outside the
      scope. The current owner's marital-status disclosure
      at the time of any future conveyance is a separate
      due-diligence step.

  X9. BANKRUPTCY-COURT MATTERS PRECEDING THE OWNER'S CURRENT
      ACQUISITION
      The bankruptcy-court search in FACT 05 was for the
      RECORDED CURRENT OWNER as a debtor. Prior owners'
      bankruptcies, where they affected title at a prior
      point in the chain, were not separately searched at
      this layer; any such prior bankruptcy is a legal
      determination based on the recorded chain only.

  X10. FEDERAL TAX LIEN PRIORITY
       Federal tax liens follow federal-law priority rules
       under 26 U.S.C. § 6323 that depend on factors
       (notice, knowledge, perfection of competing
       interests) outside the recorder's index. No federal
       tax lien was returned for this owner in this search
       (see FACT 04 L2); priority of any later-filed
       federal tax lien against any current encumbrance is
       not determined by this search.

  X11. RACIAL COVENANT IN CC1 IS UNENFORCEABLE
       The 1924 declaration in FACT 04 CC1 contains a
       racial covenant that is UNENFORCEABLE under federal
       law (42 U.S.C. § 3604; Shelley v. Kraemer, 334 U.S.
       1 (1948)) and may be independently void under the
       law of the situs state. This seal records the
       covenant's existence in the public record as a
       matter of historical fact; the covenant has no
       current legal effect. Whether the current owner has
       filed a state-specific "unlawful restrictive
       covenant" notice to formally repudiate the
       historical covenant is noted in FACT 07 if
       applicable.

  X12. SUBDIVISION-MAP AMBIGUITY (BOOK 312 PAGES 47-48)
       The recorder's certified copy of the 1923
       subdivision map shows minor degradation at the
       Lot 14 northeast corner pin notation. The officer's
       transcription in FACT 02 follows the assessor's
       current interpretation of the lot dimensions; a
       definitive answer to the corner-pin location would
       require a licensed surveyor's field verification
       and is excepted under X1.

  X13. PLANT-INDEX LAG WINDOW
       The plant-index cutoff was 1 minute behind the
       search effective date; the gap-search protocol in
       FACT 05 covered the 1-minute window. The officer
       relied on the direct recorder-index lookup for the
       gap window. The seal's effective date is the
       direct-recorder-index date, not the plant date.

WHAT EXCEPTIONS DO AND DO NOT MEAN

An exception is a matter that this search does NOT determine
either way. Some exceptions identify matters likely to be
benign in a specific transaction (e.g., X1 survey matters
when no construction is contemplated); others identify
matters that may be material and should be separately
addressed (e.g., X5 water rights when irrigation is
contemplated, X6 mineral rights when subsurface use is
contemplated). The officer makes no judgment about which
exceptions are material to which downstream use; that
judgment is the buyer's, the lender's, and the underwriter's.

The exceptions list above is NOT a checklist of all possible
defects. It is the officer's good-faith enumeration of the
known categories of out-of-scope matters as of the search
effective date. Categories may exist that the officer did
not enumerate; a category not listed here is not by that
fact insured.

This fact asserts only the exceptions enumerated above as
applicable to this fictional search. It does not assert
that the list is exhaustive of all possible out-of-scope
matters, that any specific exception will or will not be
material to any specific transaction, or that any specific
exception has any specific legal effect under the law of
the situs state.
