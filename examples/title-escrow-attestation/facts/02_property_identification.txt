WHY EFFECTIVE DATE AND COMPLETION DATE ARE BOTH RECORDED

The effective date is the as-of date of the search: it is the
date at which the recorder's index was inspected. The completion
date is the date at which the officer finished reviewing all the
returned instruments and certified the file. Any recording made
between the effective date and the completion date is by
definition OUTSIDE this search and must be addressed by a "gap
search" before commitment issuance (see FACT 05 methodology).

The seal commits to the search as of the EFFECTIVE date, not the
completion date. A subsequent buyer who relies on this seal
without running a fresh gap search forward from the effective
date is taking on uninsured gap-period risk; the seal does not
cover the gap period.
