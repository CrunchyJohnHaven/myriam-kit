POST-COMPLETION GAP NOTE

The seal commits to the search as of the EFFECTIVE date
(2026-05-19 00:00:00 PDT). Any recording in the interval from
the effective date through closing on the contemplated
transaction is OUTSIDE the search and must be covered by a
fresh gap search immediately before closing. This is standard
practice and is not a defect of this kit; the kit makes the
boundary EXPLICIT so a fresh-search obligation cannot be
quietly omitted.

WHAT THIS METHODOLOGY DOES NOT COVER

  - Survey matters (boundary location, encroachments visible
    on the land, fence-line discrepancies, party-wall
    conditions). Survey-matter exception is in FACT 06.
  - Mineral, oil, and gas estates if severed from the
    surface estate prior to the root of title (1923 in this
    chain). Pre-root mineral severances are explicitly
    outside the search.
  - Water rights, riparian rights, and prior-appropriation
    claims under state water law. Water-rights exception is
    in FACT 06.
  - Native American tribal-trust or restricted-land claims,
    where applicable to the situs (FICTIONAL — for this
    fictional parcel, no such claim has been recorded; the
    issue is jurisdiction-specific).
  - Aboriginal title and other pre-statehood claims not
    addressed by the federal patent system.
  - Off-record matters generally (unrecorded easements,
    boundary-line agreements, prescriptive easements,
    adverse possession, parol gifts of land). Off-record
    exception is in FACT 06.
  - Federal-tax-lien priority determinations (these depend
    on facts outside the recorder's index, including
    federal notices of lien with their 26 U.S.C. § 6323
    priority rules). The federal-tax-lien exception is in
    FACT 06.
  - Anything that requires personal knowledge of the
    grantor or grantee parties beyond what the recorder's
    index shows (capacity, marital status as of the date of
    conveyance, undisclosed beneficial ownership, etc.).

PHYSICAL INSPECTION

This is a record search only. The officer did not physically
visit the property and is not asserting anything about the
physical condition of the parcel, the presence of
improvements, the occupancy status, or any matter visible on
inspection that is not also recorded.

This fact asserts only the search methodology declared above,
the records covered, the date ranges searched, the title-plant
data sources used, and the gap-search protocol applied at the
search effective date. It does not assert:
  - that the methodology is the best methodology available,
  - that it complies with any particular state's title-
    examination standards (those are addressed by underwriter
    audit and licensing-board oversight),
  - that no record was missed (search is bounded by the
    indexes available to the searcher),
  - that the underlying recorder's index is itself complete
    or non-corrupted.
