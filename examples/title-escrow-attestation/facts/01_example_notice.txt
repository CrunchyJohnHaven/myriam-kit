FACT 01 — EXAMPLE NOTICE

This publication is an EXAMPLE demonstration of how a licensed
title officer, escrow agent, abstractor, or underwriter
representative can use myriam-kit to seal a point-in-time chain-of-
title attestation for a specific real-property parcel. The parcel
identifier, county, chain of recorded conveyances, encumbrances,
search methodology, exceptions, companion disclosures, and signing-
principal license details in subsequent facts are ALL FICTIONAL.

The purpose of this publication is to show the SHAPE of a real
title / escrow search seal. A real attestation would replace each
fact body with the actual artifacts of the actual search for the
actual property.

This kit is intended to compose with — not replace — the existing
title-insurance, escrow, and real-property recording regimes of
the jurisdiction in which the property sits (the ALTA commitment
and policy forms, the state insurance department's underwriter
regulation, the state real-estate-commission's licensee
regulation, the county recorder of deeds as the system of record,
applicable surveyor and environmental disclosure obligations,
etc.). The MYRIAM seal provides Bitcoin-anchored tamper-evidence
on top of the underwriter's commitment and the recorder's index.
It does NOT itself insure against loss; it does NOT detect
upstream recording fraud; it does NOT substitute for the
underwriter's indemnity. See README.md for the full set of
failure modes the seal does NOT defend against, and refusal-rules
the kit enforces.

This fact asserts only that the publication is an example, not a
real title or escrow attestation. No real property is hereby
encumbered or quieted. No real owner, lien beneficiary, or
licensee is hereby identified.
