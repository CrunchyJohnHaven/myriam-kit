WHY BOTH SIGNATORIES ARE PERSON-LEVEL (NOT ROLE-LEVEL)

In contrast to the court-evidence example in this kit family
(where forensic examiners are role-only-published), the title-
officer signing principal IS published at person level. The
reasons are:

  - The title officer's license is a personal license, not an
    agency license; the public record of the license is
    already keyed to the officer's name.
  - The underwriter's appointment is to the named officer, not
    to an anonymous role.
  - State insurance departments publish licensee rosters
    publicly; the officer's name is not private to the
    transaction in the way a forensic examiner's name is
    private to the case.
  - The accountability function of the seal depends on the
    officer being identifiable; an anonymous title officer is
    not subject to the licensure regime that backstops the
    primitive.

Personal contact information (home address, personal cell
number, family information) is NOT in the public seal even
though the name is. The agency address on file with the state
insurance department is the appropriate contact channel.

In a real attestation, the signing principal section MUST
include:

  - The agency's identity and state insurance-department
    identifier.
  - The licensed officer's name as licensed and the state
    license number (both publicly registrable against the
    state's licensee roster).
  - The underwriter of appointment.
  - A cryptographic key (PGP, X.509, or other) bound to the
    officer's license so that future statements claimed to
    be from the officer can be cryptographically verified
    against this seal.
  - A co-signatory from the underwriter authorizing the
    publication.
  - The search effective date, completion date, and seal
    date.

The signing principal section SHOULD NOT include:

  - Personal contact information for the officer (home
    address, personal cell, family information).
  - Beneficial-owner identification behind any party
    referenced in the search (protected by R1).
  - Personal identifiers of any recorded chain-of-title
    party beyond what is itself recorded (protected by R6).
  - Any party not authorized to be in the public seal under
    R1-R8.

This fact asserts only the signing principal identification
fields for the fictional search as declared. It does not
assert any of those fields about any real agency, any real
officer, any real underwriter, or any real property.
