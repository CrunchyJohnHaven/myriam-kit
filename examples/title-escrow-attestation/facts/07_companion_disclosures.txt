FACT 07 — COMPANION DISCLOSURES AND REFUSAL RECORD (FICTIONAL)

Per the MYRIAM kit's "constraint-first" design principle, this
fact records what the kit explicitly refused to seal and any
companion disclosures the title officer is obliged to publish
alongside the search record so that selective omission is
detectable. The disclosures here are not optional — leaving any
of them blank without explicit "none at search effective date"
attestation is itself a public red flag.

REFUSAL RECORD — patterns the kit refused to seal

The kit does NOT seal the following types of facts, even if
requested by the officer, the underwriter, the transacting
parties, or any other authority:

  R1. IDENTIFYING INFORMATION OF BENEFICIARIES OF LIENS HELD IN
      TRUST OR OTHERWISE PRIVACY-PRESERVING
      The kit publishes the recorded beneficiary as
      recorded. It does NOT pierce through an LLC, trust,
      undisclosed-assignee servicer arrangement, or other
      privacy-preserving structure to publish the
      beneficial owner. Many liens are recorded to
      privacy-preserving custodians for legitimate reasons
      (estranged-family judgments, support-arrears,
      abused-spouse-protected creditors, sealed-court
      matters); the kit treats these uniformly and does
      not publish through the privacy layer.

  R2. SEALING WITHOUT THE LICENSED OFFICER'S ACTUAL SIGNATURE
      A clerk, an assistant, an automated batch process,
      or any party other than the licensed title officer
      named in FACT 08 may NOT seal in the officer's
      name. The officer must be the live signer at the
      moment of sealing, using a cryptographic key bound
      to the officer's license.

  R3. SEALING PARTIAL SEARCHES AS COMPLETE
      A search of limited scope (deed-index only,
      single-county for a multi-county parcel, forward-
      only from a prior search) MUST be sealed as
      limited-scope with the scope clearly stated in
      FACT 02 (search type) and FACT 05 (methodology).
      A limited-scope search sealed as a "full" search
      is refused.

  R4. SEALING CHAIN-OF-TITLE FOR PROPERTIES WHERE THE
      SEARCHER KNOWS OF UNRESOLVED DISPUTES
      If the officer is contemporaneously aware of a
      pending or unresolved dispute affecting the chain
      (pending quiet-title, contested probate, contested
      divorce with property in issue, contested adverse
      possession, contested partition, contested
      boundary), the kit REFUSES to seal until the
      dispute is disclosed in FACT 07 C4.

  R5. SEALING IN JURISDICTIONS WHERE THE OFFICER LACKS LEGAL
      CAPACITY
      The signing officer's state of license and the
      property's state of situs MUST match, or the
      officer must affirmatively cite a multi-state
      authorization in FACT 08. A search sealed in a
      jurisdiction where the officer's license does not
      authorize title work is refused.

  R6. PERSONAL IDENTIFICATION BEYOND THE PUBLIC RECORD
      The kit publishes owner names as recorded in the
      conveyance instruments themselves. It does NOT
      supplement with date of birth, current mailing
      address, social-security number, current phone
      number, marital status, or any other personal
      identifier not in the recorded instrument. Where
      an owner is an entity, the kit publishes the entity
      name as recorded and does NOT publish individual
      members, beneficiaries, or partners beyond what is
      itself recorded.

  R7. SEALING OF A SEARCH THE OFFICER HAS NOT PERSONALLY
      REVIEWED
      The licensed officer must personally review the
      chain, exceptions, and methodology before sealing.
      A pass-through seal of unreviewed abstractor output
      or third-party search-vendor output is refused.

  R8. SEALING AS A SUBSTITUTE FOR THE UNDERWRITER'S
      COMMITMENT
      The seal is an attestation of the SEARCH; the
      underwriter's commitment is a separate contractual
      indemnity document. Fact bodies claiming the seal
      IS the commitment or that the seal binds the
      underwriter are refused.

If during search preparation any request matching R1-R8 was
made and refused, that refusal would be documented as a sealed
fact:
  "Refusal #N: party X requested sealing of [pattern]; the
   kit refused per refusal-rule R[m]."

  Refusals this search: NONE. (FICTIONAL)

This fact asserts only the companion-disclosure status and any
refusal record at the search effective date. It does not
assert that the refusal-rule list is exhaustive of all PII or
admissibility patterns, that all companion disclosures have
been identified by the officer, or that the disclosures listed
are themselves non-contestable.
