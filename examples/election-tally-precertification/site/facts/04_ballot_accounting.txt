This reconciliation prevents the "we found another box" failure mode by
fixing the cast-vs-tabulated-vs-rejected arithmetic at the certification
moment. Any post-certification claim that the ballot universe was
different from the above is publicly diffable against the sealed bytes.

The rejection reasons are aggregated by category at the jurisdiction
level. No individual voter's rejection is identifiable from these
aggregates (precinct-level rejection counts are NOT sealed here to
avoid creating small-cell PII risks in low-turnout precincts).

This fact asserts only the ballot-accounting totals as reconciled at
certification. It does not assert that any individual rejection was
correct, that the rejection categories are exhaustive, or that no
rejection-pattern bias exists.
