In a real attestation, the signing principals section should include:

  - Each signatory's legal name, party affiliation (where state law
    requires partisan balance on canvass boards), and statutory role
  - Each signatory's cryptographic key fingerprint OR a sealed
    chain-of-custody attestation that the signatory was personally
    present at the certification meeting
  - The state-mandated form-of-certification language (typically
    statutory; the exact text varies by state)

The signing principal section SHOULD NOT include:

  - Personal contact information for signatories
  - Home addresses or other locating information
  - Identifying information about signatories' family members
  - Any information that would create a personal-safety risk for the
    certifying officials, who in many jurisdictions face documented
    intimidation or threats

If a signatory is willing to be named on the public seal, naming them
is appropriate (public office, public role, public certification act).
If they decline to be named, the seal can still be valid with the
chair-only signature; the kit refuses to compel naming of unwilling
officials.

This fact asserts only the signing principal identification fields for
the fictional certification as declared. It does not assert any of
those fields about any real election official or jurisdiction.
