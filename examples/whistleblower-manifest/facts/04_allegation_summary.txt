FACT 04 — ALLEGATION SUMMARY (FICTIONAL)

The fictional Jane Doe alleges, on the basis of the evidence listed in
FACT 03, that:

  ACME_AEROSPACE (FICTIONAL) management, between 2024-Q3 and 2026-Q1,
  was aware of a specific manufacturing-tolerance defect in the
  fictional Acme-7800 component and chose not to escalate the defect
  through the company's standard quality-incident channel.

  The defect, if uncorrected on units already shipped, would, under
  conditions reproducible in laboratory testing, produce a non-trivial
  probability of in-service failure of a load-bearing element.

  Documents 1, 2, and 7 in FACT 03 are the internal quality reports
  that describe the defect. Documents 3 and 4 are the email threads in
  which management discussed (and, the allegation contends, decided
  against) escalation. Documents 5 and 6 are slide-deck photographs
  from a meeting in which the decision was internally communicated.

The allegation does NOT claim:

  - That any actual failure has occurred. The defect's in-service
    failure probability is non-trivial but not deterministic.
  - That every named individual in the documents acted with intent to
    conceal. Multiple readings of the documents are possible; the
    allegation reflects the whistleblower's reading.
  - That ACME_AEROSPACE has not taken corrective action in the period
    between the most recent document (2025-Q4) and this sealing
    (2026-05-21).

This fact asserts only the whistleblower's allegation as stated. It
does not assert that the allegation is true. The allegation is a
contestable claim about the meaning of the sealed documents in FACT 03.
The documents themselves are the load-bearing artifact.
