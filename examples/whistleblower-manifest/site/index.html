This page is a sealed publication of 8 facts that together constitute a complete whistleblower evidence-manifest seal: an example notice, provenance of evidence, manifest of evidence hashes, allegation summary, threat model, recipient verification protocol, no-silent- retraction commitment, and signing principal. The 8 facts have been hashed (SHA-256), combined into a Bitcoin-style Merkle root, and committed to the Bitcoin blockchain via OpenTimestamps against two independent calendar operators.

FACT 05 — THREAT MODEL (DECLARED) The fictional Jane Doe expects, based on her assessment of how similar disclosures have historically been responded to, that the following actions may be attempted against the disclosure described in FACTS 02–04. The MYRIAM seal of this publication is intended to defend against the specific subset of these actions noted with [SEAL]. ANTICIPATED RESPONSES BY THE NAMED ORGANIZATION: T1. [SEAL] Public dispute of the document contents. Defense: hashes in FACT 03 prove the documents the whistleblower currently holds match the bytes she had at sealing time. Any "altered after the fact" claim is publicly refutable. T2. [SEAL] Claim that documents were never sealed at the asserted time. Defense: the OpenTimestamps receipt against Bitcoin proves the manifest Merkle root existed at or before the OTS-attested time. T3. [SEAL] Claim documents are fabricated retroactively. Defense: the seal time (via OTS) plus the manifest hashes prove the document hashes existed at a specific time. Whether the content of the documents is true is still contestable, but their existence-as-bytes at the sealed time is not. T4. Pressure on the whistleblower to retract. No defense at the MYRIAM layer. Once sealed, the publication cannot be silently retracted; the whistleblower may make a separate sealed publication acknowledging retraction, but the original sealed publication remains. T5. Legal action against the whistleblower for disclosure itself. No defense at the MYRIAM layer. Whistleblower-protection law is jurisdiction-specific and outside the protocol's scope. The whistleblower must seek independent legal counsel. T6. Pressure on intermediaries (journalists, regulators) to ignore or bury the disclosure. Partial defense via IPFS pin: the publication is content-addressed and can be retrieved by any party who has the CID, independent of any single intermediary's cooperation. T7. Personal retaliation against the whistleblower. No defense at the MYRIAM layer. T8. Discrediting the whistleblower's motivations or character. No defense at the MYRIAM layer. The seal is about the documents, not about the whistleblower. ANTICIPATED RESPONSES BY INTERMEDIARIES: T9. [SEAL] Journalist or regulator receives documents in altered form from a third party not the original whistleblower. Defense: the manifest hashes let the recipient verify the bytes they receive match the bytes that were sealed. If hashes do not match, the recipient knows the documents have been altered in transit. T10. Journalist or regulator silently drops the story. No defense at the MYRIAM layer. But the sealed publication itself is now in the public IPFS network, retrievable by other parties even if the first recipient does not act. This fact asserts only the threat model as the whistleblower has declared it. It does not assert that all listed threats will materialize, that the defenses will be effective in every case, or that the unlisted threats are exhaustive.

FACT 06 — RECIPIENT PROTOCOL (DECLARED) This sealed publication is the *manifest* of evidence. The underlying documents are NOT contained in this publication. They are held by the fictional Jane Doe and transmitted separately to recipients. For any recipient (journalist, regulator, attorney) of the underlying documents, the verification protocol is: 1. Receive this sealed manifest from any trusted source, including by URL or by IPFS CID. The publication is content-addressed and does not require trusted-channel delivery. 2. Verify the manifest's own integrity: - Recompute the Merkle root from FACTS 01–08 per MYRIAM SPEC §3. - Confirm the root matches the one in this publication. - Verify the OpenTimestamps receipt against Bitcoin to confirm the manifest existed at or before the sealed time. 3. Separately, receive the underlying documents from the whistleblower by whatever channel is appropriate (Signal, SecureDrop, in-person handoff). The MYRIAM protocol does not specify this channel; it specifies only what the recipient does with documents once held. 4. For each document received, compute SHA-256 and compare to the hash in FACT 03. ALL hashes must match. If any hash differs: - The document the recipient has does not match what was sealed. - The document may have been altered in transit, or substituted. - The recipient should request retransmission via a different channel, or refuse to act on the disclosure. 5. If all hashes match, the recipient holds documents that are bytewise identical to what was sealed at the sealed time. This does NOT establish that the documents' contents are true; it establishes only that the recipient is reading the same bytes the whistleblower sealed. 6. The recipient may then independently evaluate the allegation in FACT 04 against the contents of the documents, and pursue reporting/regulatory action under their own judgment. 7. The recipient SHOULD NOT publish the underlying documents publicly without the whistleblower's consent. The sealed manifest is public; the documents are not. This fact asserts only the recipient verification protocol as declared. It does not assert that recipients will follow this protocol, that following the protocol guarantees correct action, or that any specific journalist or regulator is being contacted.

What this kit is for

This is a worked example for the Whistleblower Manifest Seal Kit. The kit defends against the specific failure mode where evidence gets buried, edited, or disputed by intermediaries between the whistleblower and the public. Historical cases the kit would have helped with:

  • Boeing 737 MAX (internal safety reports buried)
  • Theranos (lab-test concerns suppressed for years)
  • FDA inspector reports edited post-submission

The kit does NOT defend against personal retaliation, legal action against the whistleblower for disclosure itself, or intermediaries silently dropping the story. See the README's threat model.

To fork this kit for a real disclosure: read the README first. The cryptography is the easy part. Everything upstream of the cryptography (legal counsel, operational security, choice of recipient) is where the real risk lives.
