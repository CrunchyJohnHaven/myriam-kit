FACT 05 — THREAT MODEL (DECLARED)

The fictional Jane Doe expects, based on her assessment of how
similar disclosures have historically been responded to, that the
following actions may be attempted against the disclosure described
in FACTS 02–04. The MYRIAM seal of this publication is intended to
defend against the specific subset of these actions noted with [SEAL].

ANTICIPATED RESPONSES BY THE NAMED ORGANIZATION:

  T1. [SEAL] Public dispute of the document contents.
      Defense: hashes in FACT 03 prove the documents the whistleblower
      currently holds match the bytes she had at sealing time. Any
      "altered after the fact" claim is publicly refutable.

  T2. [SEAL] Claim that documents were never sealed at the asserted time.
      Defense: the OpenTimestamps receipt against Bitcoin proves the
      manifest Merkle root existed at or before the OTS-attested time.

  T3. [SEAL] Claim documents are fabricated retroactively.
      Defense: the seal time (via OTS) plus the manifest hashes prove
      the document hashes existed at a specific time. Whether the
      content of the documents is true is still contestable, but their
      existence-as-bytes at the sealed time is not.

  T4. Pressure on the whistleblower to retract.
      No defense at the MYRIAM layer. Once sealed, the publication
      cannot be silently retracted; the whistleblower may make a
      separate sealed publication acknowledging retraction, but the
      original sealed publication remains.

  T5. Legal action against the whistleblower for disclosure itself.
      No defense at the MYRIAM layer. Whistleblower-protection law is
      jurisdiction-specific and outside the protocol's scope. The
      whistleblower must seek independent legal counsel.

  T6. Pressure on intermediaries (journalists, regulators) to ignore
      or bury the disclosure.
      Partial defense via IPFS pin: the publication is
      content-addressed and can be retrieved by any party who has the
      CID, independent of any single intermediary's cooperation.

  T7. Personal retaliation against the whistleblower.
      No defense at the MYRIAM layer.

  T8. Discrediting the whistleblower's motivations or character.
      No defense at the MYRIAM layer. The seal is about the documents,
      not about the whistleblower.

ANTICIPATED RESPONSES BY INTERMEDIARIES:

  T9. [SEAL] Journalist or regulator receives documents in altered form
      from a third party not the original whistleblower.
      Defense: the manifest hashes let the recipient verify the bytes
      they receive match the bytes that were sealed. If hashes do not
      match, the recipient knows the documents have been altered in
      transit.

  T10. Journalist or regulator silently drops the story.
      No defense at the MYRIAM layer. But the sealed publication
      itself is now in the public IPFS network, retrievable by other
      parties even if the first recipient does not act.

This fact asserts only the threat model as the whistleblower has
declared it. It does not assert that all listed threats will
materialize, that the defenses will be effective in every case, or
that the unlisted threats are exhaustive.
