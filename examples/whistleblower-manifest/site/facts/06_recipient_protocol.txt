FACT 06 — RECIPIENT PROTOCOL (DECLARED)

This sealed publication is the *manifest* of evidence. The underlying
documents are NOT contained in this publication. They are held by the
fictional Jane Doe and transmitted separately to recipients.

For any recipient (journalist, regulator, attorney) of the underlying
documents, the verification protocol is:

  1. Receive this sealed manifest from any trusted source, including
     by URL or by IPFS CID. The publication is content-addressed and
     does not require trusted-channel delivery.

  2. Verify the manifest's own integrity:
     - Recompute the Merkle root from FACTS 01–08 per MYRIAM SPEC §3.
     - Confirm the root matches the one in this publication.
     - Verify the OpenTimestamps receipt against Bitcoin to confirm
       the manifest existed at or before the sealed time.

  3. Separately, receive the underlying documents from the whistleblower
     by whatever channel is appropriate (Signal, SecureDrop, in-person
     handoff). The MYRIAM protocol does not specify this channel; it
     specifies only what the recipient does with documents once held.

  4. For each document received, compute SHA-256 and compare to the
     hash in FACT 03. ALL hashes must match. If any hash differs:
     - The document the recipient has does not match what was sealed.
     - The document may have been altered in transit, or substituted.
     - The recipient should request retransmission via a different
       channel, or refuse to act on the disclosure.

  5. If all hashes match, the recipient holds documents that are
     bytewise identical to what was sealed at the sealed time. This
     does NOT establish that the documents' contents are true; it
     establishes only that the recipient is reading the same bytes
     the whistleblower sealed.

  6. The recipient may then independently evaluate the allegation in
     FACT 04 against the contents of the documents, and pursue
     reporting/regulatory action under their own judgment.

  7. The recipient SHOULD NOT publish the underlying documents publicly
     without the whistleblower's consent. The sealed manifest is
     public; the documents are not.

This fact asserts only the recipient verification protocol as declared.
It does not assert that recipients will follow this protocol, that
following the protocol guarantees correct action, or that any specific
journalist or regulator is being contacted.
