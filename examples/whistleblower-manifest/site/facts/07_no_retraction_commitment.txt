FACT 07 — NO-SILENT-RETRACTION COMMITMENT (DECLARED)

The fictional Jane Doe commits, at the moment of sealing, that:

  1. SHE WILL NOT silently retract this publication. Once sealed, this
     publication is part of the public record. If she later concludes
     that the allegation in FACT 04 is incorrect, she will make that
     conclusion a NEW sealed publication that references this one by
     Merkle root, rather than asking that this one be unpublished.

  2. SHE WILL NOT dispute the manifest hashes in FACT 03. The bytes
     she sealed are the bytes she will continue to claim are the
     authentic documents. Any apparent "I never sealed those bytes"
     statement made by her in the future should be treated as evidence
     of coercion, since the sealed record makes that statement
     impossible to make truthfully.

  3. SHE WILL acknowledge corrections. If a recipient demonstrates that
     a hash in FACT 03 is incorrect (i.e., the bytes she sealed do not
     match the bytes she now has), she will issue a new sealed
     publication acknowledging the discrepancy and explaining its
     cause.

  4. SHE WILL respect the documents-vs-allegation distinction. The
     allegation in FACT 04 is contestable. The hashes in FACT 03 are
     not contestable as bytes-at-time-of-sealing. She will not conflate
     the two in her own future statements.

This commitment is intended as a *forensic anchor*. Any future
statement by Jane Doe that contradicts this commitment can be
publicly compared against this sealed declaration, providing evidence
of either a change in her position or external coercion.

This fact asserts only the commitment as declared. It does not assert
that the commitment will be honored, or that honoring it will protect
the whistleblower from retaliation.
