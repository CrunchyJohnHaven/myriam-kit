In a real attestation, the signing-principal section should include:

  - The lead auditor's legal name and accreditation ID (where
    required by jurisdiction or by the accrediting body)
  - The audit body's accreditation reference and the certification
    standard under which the audit was conducted
  - A PGP public-key fingerprint OR equivalent cryptographic key
    binding (so future amendments, suspensions, withdrawals, or
    escalations can be cryptographically tied to the same
    principal)
  - A canonical contact channel for regulators, workers, brands,
    and journalists seeking to verify or challenge the audit

The signing-principal section SHOULD NOT include:

  - Personal phone numbers or home addresses of auditors
  - Family or dependent information about auditors (auditors at
    facilities where worker organizing is hostile may face personal
    retaliation; locating information increases that risk)
  - Trade secrets of the audit body's methodology beyond what is
    disclosed in FACT 04
  - Identifying information about individual workers, informants,
    or whistleblower sources (per FACT 07 R1, R2)

If a co-signatory is unwilling to be named on the public seal, the
seal can still be valid with the lead auditor's signature; the kit
refuses to compel naming of unwilling team members. (The lead-auditor
sign-off is the load-bearing professional act; the kit's threat model
treats it as analogous to a regulatory inspector signing an
inspection report.)

This fact asserts only the signing-principal identification fields for
the fictional audit sign-off as declared. It does not assert any of
those fields about any real audit body, audit team, or supplier; nor
does it assert that the signing principal acted free of structural
pressure described in FACT 06 and FACT 07.
