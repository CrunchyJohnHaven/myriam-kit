Per the MYRIAM kit's "constraint-first" design principle, this fact
records what the kit explicitly refused to seal and any companion
disclosures that the audit body is obliged to publish alongside the
findings so that selective omission is detectable.

REFUSAL RECORD — patterns the kit refused to seal

The kit does NOT seal the following types of facts, even if requested
by the audit body or commissioning brand:

  R1. INDIVIDUAL WORKER IDENTIFICATION
      No fact may name an interviewed worker, link a quote to a
      worker, or expose any field that could re-identify a person
      (badge number, line position, dormitory room, shift, family
      relation, demographic combination that yields N<5 in this
      facility, etc.). Worker quotes in the auditor's report use
      randomized worker tokens; the mapping is NOT sealed.

  R2. WHISTLEBLOWER OR INFORMANT SOURCES
      If any finding rests on disclosure by a specific individual
      who took risk to disclose, that individual is NEVER named or
      identified in the sealed publication. The finding may be
      sealed; the source may not. A separate sealed publication
      under a different protocol (e.g. an NGO-side report)
      handles informant attestations under different threat model.

  R3. PRECISE LOCATING INFORMATION
      No fact may include the supplier's street address, GPS
      coordinates, satellite image at resolution >1km, photograph
      that identifies the building externally, or any combination
      that yields point-source identification. The country and
      region (FACT 02) is the maximum resolution sealed.
      RATIONALE: facility identification can lead to retaliation
      against workers identified as interview participants.

  R4. CLEAN-AUDIT CERTIFICATION CLAIM
      The kit refuses to seal any framing of the form "this audit
      proves the supplier is compliant" or "no violations exist."
      The seal is of the audit-as-issued. Cleanness claims are
      epistemically unsupported by audit methodology (FACT 04
      methodology limitations).

  R5. SEAL-AS-CERTIFICATE SUBSTITUTION
      The kit refuses to seal a publication that omits FACT 04
      (methodology), FACT 06 (independence), or FACT 07 (this
      fact, including refusals). Audit findings without
      methodology + independence + refusals are not a legitimate
      MYRIAM publication under this kit.

  R6. PRE-SIGN-OFF / DRAFT AUDIT
      The kit refuses to seal a draft, preliminary, or
      management-response-pending audit. Sealing happens at
      sign-off (FACT 02 audit report date), not before.

  R7. POST-HOC FAVORABLE RESEALING
      If the audit body wishes to soften a finding after sign-off,
      the kit refuses to "amend" the seal. A revised audit
      requires a NEW sealed publication referencing this one by
      Merkle root. The original sealed audit remains permanently
      visible as the audit-as-issued.

  R8. SEALING BY ANY PARTY OTHER THAN THE AUDIT BODY
      The kit refuses to allow the commissioning brand, supplier,
      certification body, or any third party to seal "the audit"
      in lieu of the audit body. Only the audit body that signed
      the report may seal it under their principal (FACT 08).

CRITICAL HONESTY ON ADOPTION

  This kit's adoption depends on auditor cooperation. Audit bodies
  whose findings are routinely softened by commercial pressure have
  every incentive NOT to seal under a protocol that makes
  post-sign-off modifications publicly detectable. The kit is most
  useful to:
    - Auditors who want a forensic anchor against pressure to
      revise findings downstream;
    - NGOs running their own parallel investigations;
    - Brands that want a verifiable record of audits they
      commissioned;
    - Workers, worker-rights organizations, regulators, courts,
      and journalists who want to anchor a particular audit's
      contents at sign-off for later forensic comparison.
  The kit is structurally LESS useful when the dominant pressure is
  toward audit capture; in that case, the kit's primary function is
  to make refusal-to-seal a publicly visible decision (i.e.,
  refusal-to-seal becomes itself a signal).

This fact asserts only the companion-disclosure status and refusal
record at the moment of audit sign-off. It does not assert that the
refusal-rule list is exhaustive of all PII or safety patterns, that
the conflict-of-interest disclosures are exhaustive, or that
compliance with the kit's refusal rules entails substantive worker
protection.
