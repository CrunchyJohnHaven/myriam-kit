FACT 04 — METHODOLOGY AND EVIDENCE MANIFEST (FICTIONAL)

This fact records HOW the audit was conducted and what evidence the
findings in FACT 03 rest on. Sealing methodology alongside findings
prevents the "the audit was thorough" defense from being unfalsifiable
later — the methodology is part of the public record.

CRITICAL HONESTY ON METHODOLOGY:

  - The audit could not rule out hidden subcontracting, off-the-books
    workers, or production diverted to non-audited facilities during
    the audit window. These are well-documented industry-wide blind
    spots, not specific to this audit body.
  - The audit could not rule out worker coaching: 14 days advance
    notice provides time for management to script responses, remove
    underage workers, hide non-compliant equipment, and clean
    dormitories.
  - The 4% interview-sample fraction is at the upper end of industry
    norms but does NOT constitute statistical representativeness for
    rare-event findings (e.g. severe abuse of a small number of
    workers may be wholly missed).

This fact asserts only the methodology and evidence inventory as
recorded for the fictional audit. It does not assert that the
methodology is adequate to detect the abuses the standard is meant to
prevent, that the interview sample is representative of the workforce,
or that the absence of an audit finding entails the absence of the
underlying violation.
