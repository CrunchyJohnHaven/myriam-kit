FACT 05 — CORRECTIVE ACTIONS COMMITTED (FICTIONAL)

In the spirit of MYRIAM's "minimize preventable harm over time" objective,
this fact records the corrective-action plan (CAP) committed to by the
supplier and accepted by the audit body at sign-off. Sealing the CAP at
sign-off prevents later silent narrowing, deadline-extension, or recat-
egorization of major non-conformances into "closed" status without
remediation.

ESCALATION COMMITMENTS

  - If any MAJOR_NC is not closed within 90 days, the audit body
    commits to issuing a written ESCALATION notice to:
      - Supplier executive management
      - Commissioning brand (Fictional Brand A)
      - All named recipient brands (Fictional Brands B, C)
      - Accreditation body (SAAS, fictional)
    Each escalation notice will be sealed as a NEW MYRIAM publication
    referencing this one by Merkle root.

  - If a CRITICAL_NC is discovered between sign-off and re-audit
    (e.g. via worker hotline, NGO investigation, regulatory action,
    or whistleblower disclosure), the audit body commits to issuing
    a SUSPENSION notice within 5 business days of corroboration.

NO SILENT CAP-RELAXATION COMMITMENT

  If the CAP above is materially relaxed, deadlines extended, or
  non-conformances reclassified between sign-off and re-audit, that
  change will be sealed as a NEW MYRIAM publication. Replacement of a
  sealed MAJOR_NC with an unsealed "closed" status is not a legitimate
  amendment.

CRITICAL HONESTY ON CORRECTIVE ACTIONS:

  - Some root causes are outside the supplier's unilateral control.
    Excess overtime driven by brand-side order-planning practices, or
    living-wage gaps driven by brand-side price pressure, cannot be
    closed by supplier action alone. CAPs that put 100% of remediation
    burden on the supplier while the brand continues to issue order
    patterns that necessitate the violation are a documented failure
    pattern of the audit-and-remediate industry.
  - "Closed" status on a CAP item means "the auditor verified the
    document or photograph requested," not "the worker conditions
    underlying the finding have substantively improved."
  - Reaudit-without-resampling can produce false closures: if the
    re-audit reviews only the items on the CAP and not a fresh
    cross-section of the operation, regression can go undetected.

This fact asserts only the corrective-action commitments as recorded
at sign-off for the fictional audit. It does not assert that the
commitments will be honored, that honoring them will substantively
improve worker conditions, or that the audit-and-remediate model is
adequate to the harms it nominally addresses.
