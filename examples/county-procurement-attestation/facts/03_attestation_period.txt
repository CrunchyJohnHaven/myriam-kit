FACT 03 — ATTESTATION PERIOD (FICTIONAL)

This fact defines the temporal scope of the attestation: the exact
calendar quarter being sealed, the moment the quarterly procurement
books were formally closed by the Procurement Director, and the
moment the seal was created. Sealing the temporal scope at the
moment of sealing prevents post-facto reattribution of an attestation
to a different quarter or framing of partial-quarter data as a full-
quarter close — both of which are documented failure modes in county
procurement transparency.

  Calendar quarter:        2026-Q2 (FICTIONAL)
  Period start (00:00):    2026-04-01 00:00:00 UTC (FICTIONAL)
  Period end (23:59):      2026-06-30 23:59:59 UTC (FICTIONAL)
  Calendar basis:          Calendar-year quarters (most counties
                           operate on calendar-year quarterly close;
                           counties on fiscal-year quarters
                           (commonly July–June in some states)
                           should substitute the fiscal-quarter dates
                           and note the fiscal-year basis here)

  Quarter-close memorandum co-signed (CFO):
                           2026-07-14T17:15:00 UTC (FICTIONAL)
  Quarter-close memorandum SHA-256:
    a1b2c3d4e5f6789abcdef0123456789abcdef0123456789abcdef0123456789ab

  The quarter-close memorandum is the procurement office's internal
  declaration that all procurement activity issued during the quarter
  has been entered into the procurement system of record, that the
  general-ledger reconciliation has been performed by the CFO's
  office, and that the quarterly procurement record is now closed
  for editing. Any later edit to the closed quarterly record is
  itself a procurement event that must be sealed under a SEPARATE
  attestation (a "post-close amendment" attestation), not silently
  absorbed into the closed quarter's record.

ATTESTATION SCOPE CONSTRAINTS

  This attestation covers procurement activity ISSUED during the
  named calendar quarter, defined as activity with a notice-to-
  proceed date, change-order issuance date, or disbursement date
  falling within 2026-04-01 through 2026-06-30 inclusive.

  Procurement activity recorded after the close date but with an
  issuance date within the quarter (e.g., a 2026-06-29 notice-to-
  proceed entered into the system on 2026-07-08) IS included.

  Procurement activity with an issuance date outside the quarter
  but recorded into the system during the quarter (e.g., a backdated
  contract issued in March but entered in April) is NOT included
  under the issuance-date scope, but IS disclosed in FACT 08 as a
  separate item ("transactions entered this quarter but issued
  outside the quarter") so that the bookkeeping discrepancy is on
  the record.

  Procurement activity routed through joint-powers authorities,
  county-related public-benefit corporations, special districts,
  or state pass-through grants is OUT OF SCOPE for this attestation;
  it should be sealed under separate attestations by the entities
  that issued it.

REFUSAL: PARTIAL-QUARTER OR ROLLED-FORWARD ATTESTATION

  The kit refuses to seal an attestation framed as a full-quarter
  close that in fact covers only part of the quarter, OR a previously
  sealed quarterly attestation re-sealed with new content under the
  same calendar-quarter label. A re-sealed quarter is itself a
  procurement event and must be sealed as a NEW attestation with a
  new attestation type ("quarterly procurement attestation, amended")
  pointing back to the original attestation's Merkle root.

This fact asserts only the temporal scope of the attestation: which
calendar quarter is covered, when it was formally closed, and when
it was sealed. It does not assert that all procurement activity
issued during the named quarter is fully captured in the
attestation (only that which was entered into the procurement system
of record by the close date is captured), that the close was
performed without irregularity, or that the gap between close and
sealing was free of modification activity.
