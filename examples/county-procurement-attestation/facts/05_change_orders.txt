FACT 05 — CHANGE ORDERS (FICTIONAL)

This is the second of the two load-bearing content facts of the seal.
Change orders are the dominant unit of post-award price inflation in
county procurement: a contract may be cleanly awarded under
competitive bid and then silently inflated 30–60% through post-award
change orders that never see competitive scrutiny. Anchoring each
change order at the moment of issuance — with reason code, cost
impact, and cumulative-change-as-percent-of-original — makes change-
order creep publicly diffable in advance of audit.

Any later silent revision to any change-order amount, reason, or
date below would produce a different SHA-256 of this fact file, a
different Merkle root for this publication, and would fail
verification against the Bitcoin-committed root.

This fact asserts only the change-order events as recorded by the
county procurement office at the moment of issuance, with the
contemporaneous reason codes, cost impacts, cumulative-change
percentages, and approving authorities as they appeared on the
issuance date. It does not assert that any change order was
substantively justified by the conditions cited; that any cumulative-
change-creep pattern is or is not the result of an underlying
corruption pattern (such as low-balling the initial bid and recouping
through change orders); that the recorded reason codes accurately
characterize the underlying engineering or scope facts; or that the
change-order approval process was free of conflict of interest. The
seal anchors WHAT WAS REPORTED on the day of issuance; whether the
underlying scope changes were legitimate is an audit and IG matter,
not a sealing matter.
