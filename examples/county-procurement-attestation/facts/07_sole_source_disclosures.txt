FACT 07 — SOLE-SOURCE DISCLOSURES (FICTIONAL)

This fact records every sole-source award and every structural sole-
source-equivalent (professional-services exemption, emergency
procurement, state-contract piggyback under specific conditions,
designated-nonprofit-only awards) issued during the attestation
quarter. Each entry includes the statutory citation, the date the
sole-source justification was filed, the SHA-256 of the justification
document, and the timestamp at which the justification was noticed
to the county Inspector General.

Sole-source procurement is one of the two highest-risk categories in
county purchasing (change orders being the other). The defense
against sole-source abuse is NOT prohibition (legitimate sole-source
situations exist — patented technology, designated nonprofits,
incumbent IT systems with switching costs that exceed re-bid value)
but PROCEDURAL DISCIPLINE around the disclosure: justification on
the record, statutory citation, IG notice, and post-facto audit.
Sealing the sole-source disclosure at issuance makes the procedural-
discipline trail permanent.

This fact asserts only the sole-source and sole-source-equivalent
awards as recorded by the county procurement office, with the
statutory citations, justification document hashes, IG-notice
timestamps, and selection-process details as they appeared on the
issuance date. It does not assert that any sole-source justification
was substantively valid; that no qualified competitive bidder existed
for any sole-source award; that the periodic-review cycles are
adequate to catch outdated sole-source designations; that the
emergency that triggered the emergency procurement was itself
genuine and not pretextual; or that the professional-services
exemption was not used to avoid scrutiny of an otherwise-bid-eligible
service. The seal anchors WHAT WAS DISCLOSED. Investigation of
WHETHER THE DISCLOSURE WAS HONEST is the jurisdiction of the IG,
the state auditor, federal grant managers, and prosecutors.
