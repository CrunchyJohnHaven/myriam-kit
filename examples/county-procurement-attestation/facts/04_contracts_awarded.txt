FACT 04 — CONTRACTS AWARDED (FICTIONAL)

This is one of the two load-bearing content facts of the seal (FACT 04
contracts + FACT 05 change orders together capture the procurement-
cycle signal). It records, per contract awarded during the attestation
quarter, the as-issued contract record: vendor entity, contract amount,
award method, notice-to-proceed date, and contract document hash.

Any later silent revision to any vendor name, amount, award method,
date, or document hash below would produce a different SHA-256 of this
fact file, a different Merkle root for this publication, and would fail
verification against the Bitcoin-committed root.

VENDOR IDENTIFICATION POLICY (READ FIRST)

  This fact identifies vendors by REGISTERED LEGAL ENTITY NAME and
  jurisdiction-issued business identifier (state-of-incorporation
  registration ID, NOT the vendor's federal Employer Identification
  Number — EINs of small / sole-proprietor vendors can de-anonymize
  natural persons, and are HASHED in FACT 05 rather than published).

  This fact does NOT identify:
    - Individual vendor employees, officers, or beneficial owners
    - Personal phone numbers, email addresses, or home addresses
    - Vendor bank account numbers, federal tax IDs of natural
      persons, or other personal financial information
    - Vendor names on contracts BELOW the USD 10,000 itemization
      threshold (see FACT 06's aggregate disclosure for below-
      threshold spending)

  Beneficial-ownership disclosure of vendor entities is a SEPARATE
  oversight regime (in the U.S., this includes the federal Corporate
  Transparency Act's beneficial-ownership reporting to FinCEN, plus
  state-level disclosure regimes where applicable). The seal of
  beneficial ownership is a parallel attestation and is not included
  here.

ITEMIZATION THRESHOLD

  Contracts at or above USD 10,000 are itemized in this fact, by
  vendor name + amount + award method + notice-to-proceed date +
  contract document hash. Contracts below USD 10,000 are aggregated
  in FACT 06's below-threshold category totals.

  The USD 10,000 threshold is set above the typical county petty-
  purchase / micro-purchase authority (which is commonly USD 3,500
  or USD 10,000 under 2 CFR Part 200 for federally funded counties)
  precisely so that small-dollar awards to local vendors do not
  de-anonymize individual sole proprietors.

This fact asserts only the contract-award events as recorded by the
county procurement office at the moment of issuance. It does not
assert that any contract was awarded to the most qualified bidder;
that any sole-source justification was substantively valid; that any
emergency procurement was preceded by an actual emergency; that
vendor entities are not shell companies or fronts for undisclosed
beneficial owners; that pricing was at fair market value; or that
any underlying procurement was free of corruption, collusion, bid-
rigging, or kickbacks. The seal anchors WHAT WAS REPORTED.
Investigation of WHAT WAS TRUE is the jurisdiction of the county IG,
the state auditor, federal grant managers, prosecutors, and courts.
