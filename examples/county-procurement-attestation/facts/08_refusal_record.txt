FACT 08 — REFUSAL RECORD AND SIGNING PRINCIPALS (FICTIONAL)

Per the MYRIAM kit's "constraint-first" design principle, this fact
records what the kit explicitly refused to seal during the attestation
period, the companion disclosures that the issuing authority is
obliged to publish alongside the procurement record so that selective
omission is detectable, and the named signing principals with
explicit scope of what each is and is not personally attesting to.

In county procurement the dominant attack on the integrity of
disclosure is NOT outright fabrication — it is selective publication:
posting the clean awards, omitting the messy ones, omitting the
ongoing investigations, omitting the pending protests, redacting the
vendor names on awards that "happen to be politically inconvenient."
Sealing the refusal record alongside the procurement activity makes
selective omission detectable as a deviation from the as-sealed
record.

REFUSAL RECORD — patterns the kit refused to seal during the period

The kit does NOT seal the following types of facts, even if requested
by the procurement office, the CFO, the IG, or any external party:

  R1. VENDOR NAMES OF CONTRACTS BELOW USD 10,000
      The kit refuses to itemize vendor names on contracts below
      the USD 10,000 itemization threshold. Vendors below threshold
      are aggregated by category in FACT 06's below-threshold
      section. This refusal protects sole proprietors and small
      local vendors (the local locksmith, the local towing
      service, individual professional consultants) from
      de-anonymization without serving an anti-corruption
      purpose. (The bid-splitting detection signal is preserved
      via FACT 06's per-vendor aggregate across all contracts.)

  R2. PERSONAL FINANCIAL INFORMATION OF VENDORS
      The kit refuses to seal vendor bank account numbers,
      federal tax IDs of natural persons, individual vendor-
      employee names, vendor home addresses, supplier internal
      cost structures, or any commercial-PII field whose
      publication harms vendor interests without serving anti-
      corruption. Such information may be HASHED (FACT 04 contract
      document hash includes the full executed contract including
      these fields) but not published.

  R3. IDENTITIES OF WHISTLEBLOWER IG COMPLAINANTS
      The kit refuses to identify any individual who has filed
      a complaint with the County Inspector General under
      whistleblower-protection statutes. The IG's case state
      is recorded in FACT 06 by case number and category only.
      This refusal is ABSOLUTE: even if the IG itself has
      lawfully unsealed a complainant identity in connection
      with a specific case, this kit's quarterly attestation
      does not include such identification.

  R4. SEALING BY ANYONE NOT THE ACTUAL PROCUREMENT OFFICER
      A contractor, vendor, county commissioner, citizen
      watchdog, journalist, or observer cannot seal "the
      county's procurement record" in lieu of the Procurement
      Director who is statutorily responsible for the quarterly
      attestation. (Such third parties may seal their OWN
      observations under their own principal, with their own
      refusal record, in a separate publication.)

  R5. PARTIAL-QUARTER DATA PRESENTED AS COMPLETE
      The kit refuses to seal a publication framed as a full-
      quarter close that in fact covers only part of the
      quarter, or that omits transactions issued before the
      close date but recorded after. The attestation must
      reflect all procurement activity issued during the full
      quarter as recorded at the moment of close.

  R6. CLAIMS THAT THE PROCUREMENT WAS LAWFUL, FAIR, OR
      CORRUPTION-FREE
      The kit refuses to seal any framing of the form "this
      quarter's procurement was lawful and free of corruption"
      or "all sole-source justifications were demonstrably
      valid." Such claims are epistemically beyond the kit's
      domain. The kit anchors WHAT WAS RECORDED; it does not
      vouch for the underlying procurement.

  R7. PUBLICATION OMITTING THIS FACT 08 REFUSAL RECORD
      The kit refuses to seal a publication that includes
      FACT 04 through FACT 07 but omits FACT 08. A publication
      without the refusal record is not a legitimate MYRIAM
      publication under this kit. The refusal record is the
      structural defense against selective sealing.

Public officials in named public-office roles ARE properly identified
in this fact (it is the load-bearing accountability hook of the
seal), but private information about those officials in their
private capacities is NOT.

This fact asserts only the companion-disclosure status, refusal
record, and signing-principal identification fields for the
attestation as declared at the moment of sealing. It does not assert
that the disclosure list is exhaustive of all material companion
facts that SHOULD have been disclosed, that the refusal list is
exhaustive of all attempted misuses of the kit, that absence of a
disclosure implies absence of the underlying condition, or that the
named signatories' independent-attestation declarations are
themselves immune to coercion. A more honest seal discloses more;
the seal cannot compel honesty about what is not disclosed.

The criminal-misstatement provisions referenced in this fact apply
ONLY in a real attestation by a real official under real statutory
authority; this fictional example imposes no such accountability
on any real person.
