This fact binds the entire sealed publication to a named, accountable
county procurement office acting in official capacity under a specific
state-level statutory mandate. Naming the issuing office, the FIPS
county code, and the dual-control + independent-IG signing principals
is the load-bearing referent that lets later truth-finding processes
(state auditor review, IG investigation, FOI litigation, federal grant
audits) know which legal regime applies and which named principals are
accountable.

This fact asserts only the identity of the issuing county procurement
office, the FIPS county identifier, and the statutory roles of the
named attesting principals. It does not assert that the underlying
procurement authority was correctly invoked in any specific transaction,
that the named officials' attestations were free of error or coercion,
or that the cited county / state / federal procurement statutes were
substantively complied with in the underlying procurement activity.
