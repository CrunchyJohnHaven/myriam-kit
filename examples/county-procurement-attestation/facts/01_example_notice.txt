FACT 01 — EXAMPLE NOTICE

This publication is an EXAMPLE demonstration of how a U.S. county
procurement office can use myriam-kit to seal a quarterly procurement
attestation at the moment the quarter formally closes. The county name,
FIPS code, statutory references, contract identifiers, vendor entity
names, award amounts, change-order details, sole-source justifications,
inspector-general case identifiers, and signing officials are ALL
FICTIONAL.

The purpose: show the SHAPE of a real county procurement attestation
seal. A real attestation would replace each fact body with the actual
fields of the actual procurement activity in the named county during
the named calendar quarter, as recorded by the county procurement
office at the formal quarterly-close moment.

This kit is intended to compose with — not replace — existing county
procurement infrastructure (county purchasing ordinances, state
auditor oversight, county inspector-general offices, county auditor
offices, state open-records / sunshine law regimes, federal grant
compliance regimes under 2 CFR Part 200, open-checkbook / open-data
portals, the Open Contracting Data Standard, and equivalent state-
level transparency portals). The MYRIAM seal provides Bitcoin-
anchored tamper-evidence on top of whatever publication regime the
county already operates.

The seal anchors WHAT WAS REPORTED AT THE QUARTERLY CLOSE. It does
not anchor whether the underlying procurement activity was lawful,
free of corruption, priced at market rate, or delivered as
specified. Those are downstream questions for the county IG, the
state auditor, federal grant managers, prosecutors, courts,
journalists, and citizen watchdogs. The seal makes the as-reported
state of the procurement record permanent and publicly diffable
against later versions.

DISTINCTION FROM THE BROADER public-spending-attestation/ KIT

  This kit is U.S.-county-specific and scopes to the procurement-
  cycle signals (award method, change orders, sole-source
  disclosures, vendor payment aggregates, IG case state) that are
  the specific failure surfaces in U.S. county procurement. The
  broader public-spending-attestation/ kit is jurisdiction-agnostic
  and emphasizes appropriation execution against a budget
  instrument. A county may run both, anchoring its budget
  execution under the broader kit and its procurement-cycle
  activity under this one, with cross-references between the two
  Merkle roots.

This fact asserts only that the publication is an example, not a real
county procurement attestation. No real county procurement is hereby
attested. No real official, vendor, or county is hereby named.
