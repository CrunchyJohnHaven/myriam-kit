FACT 06 — VENDOR PAYMENTS (FICTIONAL)

This fact records the aggregate cash actually paid out to each vendor
during the attestation quarter, AGGREGATED across all contracts
(this-quarter awards + prior-quarter awards + prior-year multi-year
contracts). The vendor-payment aggregate is a different signal than
the contract-award manifest in FACT 04:

  - A vendor with 6 small contracts under the bid threshold and a
    payment aggregate of USD 4.2M is a different risk pattern
    (bid splitting) than a vendor with 1 contract at USD 4.2M.
  - A vendor with one prior-year contract receiving an unusually
    large payment in the quarter signals either a milestone close-
    out or an undisclosed change order; either way it deserves
    visibility.
  - A vendor with very high aggregate payments who does not appear
    in FACT 04 (contracts awarded this quarter) is being paid
    against prior-year contracts only; the aggregate is the only
    signal that surfaces them.

Sealing per-vendor payment aggregates makes all three patterns
publicly diffable in advance of audit. The CFO co-signs this fact
specifically: the CFO attests that the per-vendor aggregates
reconcile to the county's general-ledger disbursement records for
the attestation period.

VENDOR-IDENTIFICATION POLICY

  Same as FACT 04: registered legal entity name + state-of-
  incorporation registration ID only. No federal EINs of natural
  persons. No individual employees. No personal financial
  information.

ITEMIZATION THRESHOLD FOR VENDOR PAYMENTS

  Vendors with quarterly payment aggregate ≥ USD 10,000 are
  itemized below. Below-threshold vendors are aggregated by
  spending category at the bottom of this fact.

  This is the same threshold as FACT 04 contract awards, BUT
  applied to the aggregate of payments across all contracts to
  the vendor during the quarter — not to individual payment
  events. A vendor receiving four USD 4,000 payments in the
  quarter (aggregate USD 16,000) IS itemized here even though
  no individual payment exceeded the threshold and no contract
  to that vendor would itself appear in FACT 04. This is the
  bid-splitting-detection mechanism.

This fact asserts only the per-vendor payment aggregates as recorded
in the county's general-ledger disbursement system for the
attestation period. It does not assert that the work paid for was
actually performed at the value paid; that the vendors named are
unrelated to each other or to any undisclosed beneficial owner; that
the below-threshold category aggregates represent legitimate small-
dollar consumption rather than a bid-splitting pattern; or that the
CFO's reconciliation is itself free of misclassification. The seal
anchors WHAT WAS PAID. Investigation of WHO WAS PAID FOR WHAT IN
RETURN FOR WHAT is the jurisdiction of the county IG, the state
auditor, federal grant managers, and prosecutors.
