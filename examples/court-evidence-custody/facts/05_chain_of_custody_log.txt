WHAT THIS LOG IS AND IS NOT

  This log is the agency's own contemporaneous custody record, with
  the role/name redaction applied for public publication. It is not
  a court's finding that the custody chain was procedurally correct
  under applicable law. The log is the agency's claim; whether the
  claim satisfies a state's chain-of-custody admissibility
  requirement is a matter for the court.

  Any post-sealing modification of the custody log — adding a custody
  event after the fact, removing one, or changing the timestamp on
  one — produces a different SHA-256 over this fact body and is
  publicly detectable. The hash anchor freezes the agency's
  contemporaneous custody claim at the moment of acquisition.

This fact asserts only the custody-event timeline above and the hashes
of the separately-sealed companion records, as the agency
contemporaneously recorded them at the moment of sealing. It does not
assert:
  - that the chain-of-custody was procedurally correct under
    applicable law,
  - that the role-to-individual binding is correct in the companion
    record (that is a separate evidentiary question),
  - that no custody events occurred that were not logged.
