FACT 01 — EXAMPLE NOTICE

This publication is an EXAMPLE demonstration of how a forensic
acquisition team — computer forensics examiner, mobile-device
extraction technician, body-cam evidence custodian, etc. — can use
myriam-kit to seal a chain-of-custody anchor at the moment of digital
evidence acquisition. The case identifier, warrant citation, acquiring
agency, examiner role, exhibit hashes, custody-event timeline, and all
other particulars in subsequent facts are ALL FICTIONAL.

The purpose of this publication is to show the SHAPE of a real
forensic-acquisition seal. A real attestation would replace each fact
body with the actual artifacts of the actual acquisition.

This kit is intended to compose with — not replace — the existing
chain-of-custody, evidence-handling, and admissibility procedures of
the jurisdiction running the case (Federal Rules of Evidence, Daubert
standards under FRE 702, agency forensic SOPs, defense-discovery
obligations, etc.). The MYRIAM seal provides Bitcoin-anchored
tamper-evidence on top of whatever paper-and-signature chain-of-custody
record the agency already maintains. It does NOT itself satisfy any
court's admissibility test; admissibility is a legal-procedure
question, not a cryptographic one.

This fact asserts only that the publication is an example, not a real
forensic-acquisition record. No real case is hereby investigated. No
real suspect, defendant, victim, witness, or third party is hereby
identified.
