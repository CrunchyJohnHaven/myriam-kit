records what the kit explicitly refused to seal and any companion
disclosures the acquiring agency is obliged to publish alongside the
acquisition record so that selective omission is detectable. The
disclosures here are not optional — leaving any of them blank without
explicit "none at acquisition time" attestation is itself a public
red flag.

REFUSAL RECORD — patterns the kit refused to seal

The kit does NOT seal the following types of facts, even if requested
by the acquiring agency or a co-equal authority:

  R1. INDIVIDUAL IDENTIFICATION OF SUSPECTS, DEFENDANTS, VICTIMS,
      WITNESSES, OR THIRD PARTIES
      No fact published here may name, or include any identifier
      reducible to, any individual who is a subject of, victim of,
      or witness to the investigation. The case docket reference in
      FACT 02 is the appropriate handle for parties with standing
      to locate the case in court records; this public seal is not
      a re-identification index.

  R2. PERSONAL NAMES OF FORENSIC EXAMINERS OR OTHER AGENCY STAFF
      Per FACT 06, roles are published; personal names are sealed
      separately under agency CMS access. This refusal protects
      examiner-personal-safety; agencies that find this refusal
      operationally incompatible should publish role-only at this
      layer and bind names through normal discovery.

  R3. ANY HASH OR FINGERPRINT OF SUSPECTED CHILD SEXUAL ABUSE
      MATERIAL, BIOMETRIC TEMPLATE, OR OTHER VULNERABLE-PERSON-
      RE-IDENTIFYING DATA
      Even by hash. Even by file-level fingerprint. The reason: an
      individual file's SHA-256 is a permanent searchable
      identifier; publishing it on a public ledger can re-victimize
      subjects via correlation with private databases held by other
      parties. Image-level (forensic-image-level) hashes are
      acceptable because the forensic-image binary is not itself a
      re-victimizing artifact in the same way; file-level hashes of
      such material are NEVER acceptable in a public seal.

  R4. EXAMINATION FINDINGS, INTERPRETATIONS, OR OPINIONS
      The kit anchors WHAT BYTES were acquired and WHEN. It does
      NOT anchor what the examiner concluded the bytes mean. The
      examiner's report is appropriately sealed and discovered
      under normal court process; pre-publishing a forensic
      interpretation as a tamper-evident "fact" gives the
      interpretation a misleading veneer of cryptographic
      certainty.

  R5. CLAIMS OF ADMISSIBILITY OR LEGAL CONCLUSION
      The kit will not seal a fact body that claims the evidence
      is "admissible," that the warrant was "lawful," that the
      chain-of-custody was "proper under [statute X]," or any
      other legal conclusion. Admissibility is a court function;
      the kit anchors bytes and timestamps only.

  R6. SEALING BY ANYONE OTHER THAN THE ACQUIRING AGENCY'S
      AUTHORIZED PRINCIPAL
      A defense expert, court-appointed neutral, or third party
      may use the same kit to seal THEIR OWN independent
      verification of the manifest; but they may not seal the
      ORIGINAL acquisition under the acquiring agency's name. The
      principal in FACT 08 is the only valid signer for that.

  R7. ROLE-TO-INDIVIDUAL BINDINGS IN THE PUBLIC SEAL
      As described in FACT 06, the role-to-individual binding is
      sealed separately and discoverable under court process. The
      kit refuses to publish that binding here.

  R8. PRE-SEIZURE OR PRE-WARRANT FACT PATTERNS
      The kit refuses to seal facts about the investigation that
      pre-date the warrant cited in FACT 02 (informant
      statements, pre-warrant surveillance, parallel-construction
      inputs, etc.). The seal is scoped to the acquisition event;
      pre-acquisition investigative facts have their own
      evidentiary regime and do not belong on this seal.

If during acquisition any request matching R1-R8 was made and refused,
that refusal would be documented as a sealed fact:
  "Refusal #N: party X requested sealing of [pattern]; the kit
  refused per refusal-rule R[m]."

  Refusals this acquisition: NONE. (FICTIONAL)

This fact asserts only the companion-disclosure status and any
refusal record at the moment of acquisition. It does not assert that
the refusal-rule list is exhaustive of all PII or admissibility
patterns, that all companion disclosures have been identified by the
acquiring agency, or that the disclosures listed are themselves
non-contestable.
