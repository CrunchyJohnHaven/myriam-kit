FACT 06 — EXAMINER QUALIFICATIONS BY ROLE (FICTIONAL)

This fact records, at role level, the certifications and qualifications
of the personnel who performed the acquisition described in FACTS 02
through 05. PERSONAL NAMES ARE NOT IN THIS PUBLIC SEAL. The role-to-
individual binding is sealed in the separately-held companion record
hashed in FACT 05. The reason: personal names of forensic examiners
have, in past cases, been used to harass examiners, attempt to coerce
them outside courtroom procedure, or build a searchable index of
which examiner worked which cases that aids organized opposition.
Examiner credentials are public; examiner-personal-identification per
case is appropriately discoverable under court process, not on a
permanent public ledger.

WHAT THIS FACT DOES AND DOES NOT ASSERT

  This fact records the credential profile that the roles' personnel
  held at the time of acquisition. It is intended to be cross-
  checked against the certifying bodies' public registries (IACIS,
  SANS GIAC, Magnet Forensics) using the credentials listed, NOT
  using personal names. Verification at this layer answers the
  question "was the work done by a person with active credentials of
  this type" but does not answer "was THIS specific person, with
  this specific history, qualified to do THIS specific work." That
  second question is for the court at any Daubert challenge.

This fact asserts only the role-level certifications and conflict-of-
interest disclosures as they stood at the time of acquisition. It
does not assert:
  - that certification implies competence in any specific case,
  - that an absence of a disclosed conflict means no conflict exists,
  - that the examiner's prior expert-witness qualification in other
    proceedings binds any future court to qualify them in this case,
  - that the role-to-individual binding (sealed separately, hashed
    in FACT 05) is itself uncontested.
