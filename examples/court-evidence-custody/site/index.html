See README.md for the full set of failure modes the seal does NOT defend against, and refusal-rules the kit enforces.

This page is a sealed publication of 8 facts that together constitute a complete forensic-acquisition chain-of-custody seal: an example notice, acquisition context, evidence manifest, acquisition methodology, chain-of-custody event log, examiner qualifications by role, companion disclosures and refusal record, and signing principal. The 8 facts have been hashed (SHA-256), combined into a Bitcoin-style Merkle root, and committed to the Bitcoin blockchain via OpenTimestamps against two independent calendar operators.

The Eight Facts

FACT 01 — EXAMPLE NOTICE This publication is an EXAMPLE demonstration of how a forensic acquisition team — computer forensics examiner, mobile-device extraction technician, body-cam evidence custodian, etc. — can use myriam-kit to seal a chain-of-custody anchor at the moment of digital evidence acquisition. The case identifier, warrant citation, acquiring agency, examiner role, exhibit hashes, custody-event timeline, and all other particulars in subsequent facts are ALL FICTIONAL. The purpose of this publication is to show the SHAPE of a real forensic-acquisition seal. A real attestation would replace each fact body with the actual artifacts of the actual acquisition. This kit is intended to compose with — not replace — the existing chain-of-custody, evidence-handling, and admissibility procedures of the jurisdiction running the case (Federal Rules of Evidence, Daubert standards under FRE 702, agency forensic SOPs, defense-discovery obligations, etc.). The MYRIAM seal provides Bitcoin-anchored tamper-evidence on top of whatever paper-and-signature chain-of-custody record the agency already maintains. It does NOT itself satisfy any court's admissibility test; admissibility is a legal-procedure question, not a cryptographic one. This fact asserts only that the publication is an example, not a real forensic-acquisition record. No real case is hereby investigated. No real suspect, defendant, victim, witness, or third party is hereby identified.

ANY HASH OR FINGERPRINT OF SUSPECTED CHILD SEXUAL ABUSE MATERIAL, BIOMETRIC TEMPLATE, OR OTHER VULNERABLE-PERSON- RE-IDENTIFYING DATA Even by hash. Even by file-level fingerprint. The reason: an individual file's SHA-256 is a permanent searchable identifier; publishing it on a public ledger can re-victimize subjects via correlation with private databases held by other parties. Image-level (forensic-image-level) hashes are acceptable because the forensic-image binary is not itself a re-victimizing artifact in the same way; file-level hashes of such material are NEVER acceptable in a public seal. R4. EXAMINATION FINDINGS, INTERPRETATIONS, OR OPINIONS The kit anchors WHAT BYTES were acquired and WHEN. It does NOT anchor what the examiner concluded the bytes mean. The examiner's report is appropriately sealed and discovered under normal court process; pre-publishing a forensic interpretation as a tamper-evident "fact" gives the interpretation a misleading veneer of cryptographic certainty. R5. CLAIMS OF ADMISSIBILITY OR LEGAL CONCLUSION The kit will not seal a fact body that claims the evidence is "admissible," that the warrant was "lawful," that the chain-of-custody was "proper under [statute X]," or any other legal conclusion. Admissibility is a court function; the kit anchors bytes and timestamps only. R6. SEALING BY ANYONE OTHER THAN THE ACQUIRING AGENCY'S AUTHORIZED PRINCIPAL A defense expert, court-appointed neutral, or third party may use the same kit to seal THEIR OWN independent verification of the manifest; but they may not seal the ORIGINAL acquisition under the acquiring agency's name. The principal in FACT 08 is the only valid signer for that. R7. ROLE-TO-INDIVIDUAL BINDINGS IN THE PUBLIC SEAL As described in FACT 06, the role-to-individual binding is sealed separately and discoverable under court process. The kit refuses to publish that binding here. R8. PRE-SEIZURE OR PRE-WARRANT FACT PATTERNS The kit refuses to seal facts about the investigation that pre-date the warrant cited in FACT 02 (informant statements, pre-warrant surveillance, parallel-construction inputs, etc.). The seal is scoped to the acquisition event; pre-acquisition investigative facts have their own evidentiary regime and do not belong on this seal. What this kit is for

This is a worked example for the Court Evidence Chain-of-Custody Seal Kit. The kit anchors WHAT BYTES were acquired and WHEN, by WHICH AGENCY, under WHICH WARRANT, through WHICH TOOLS — at the moment of forensic acquisition. It is intended to compose with, not replace, existing chain-of-custody and admissibility procedures.

What the kit does NOT establish:

  • Admissibility under FRE 901 / FRE 702 / Daubert / state evidence codes
  • Lawfulness of the warrant or the seizure
  • Whether the evidence is incriminating or exculpatory
  • Procedural correctness of the chain-of-custody under applicable law
  • Reliability of the forensic tools or methodology in any specific case

The kit refuses to seal personal identification of any party, file-level hashes of vulnerable-person re-identifying material, examination findings or legal conclusions. See the README for the full set of refusal rules.

To fork this kit for a real acquisition: read the README first. The cryptography is the easy part. The agency's existing chain-of-custody discipline, the warrant's lawfulness, and the methodology's reliability all live upstream of the cryptography.
