FACT 05 — SUPPORTING DOCUMENT HASHES (FICTIONAL)

This fact anchors the supporting documentation behind each transaction
in FACT 04 by content hash, NOT by full content. The hashes-only design
serves two purposes:

  (1) Document bodies routinely contain personal information of
      contractor employees (signatures, drivers' license numbers,
      site-staff lists), supplier banking information, internal
      pricing breakdowns, and protected commercial information. Sealing
      hashes anchors the bytes-as-issued without publishing the bytes.

  (2) A regulator, auditor, journalist, or court with lawful access to
      the original document can re-compute SHA-256 of the received
      document and confirm bit-for-bit identity with the document that
      existed at the moment of attestation. Silent post-attestation
      modification of any supporting document is detectable by re-hash.

Each hash below is SHA-256 of the full canonical document file, computed
at the moment of sealing.

A regulator, inspector general, auditor, journalist, or court with
lawful access to any of the above documents can re-hash the received
document with SHA-256 and compare to the row above. Any post-issuance
modification of any document produces a different hash than the sealed
one and is publicly detectable.

This fact asserts only that the listed supporting documents existed in
their hashed bytewise form at the moment of attestation. It does not
assert that the documents are themselves accurate, complete, or
non-misleading; that the procurement processes they document complied
with applicable law in substance; or that the costs and quantities they
contain reflect work actually performed at fair value.
