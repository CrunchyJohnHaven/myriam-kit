Public Spending Attestation — Example Seal

Sealed under the MYRIAM-kit protocol · MYRIAM-kit · EXAMPLE — FICTIONAL

This page is a sealed publication of 8 facts under the MYRIAM-kit protocol. Each fact below has been hashed (SHA-256), combined into a Bitcoin-style Merkle root, and committed to the Bitcoin blockchain via OpenTimestamps against two independent calendar operators. Any change to any fact would produce a Merkle root different from the one committed to Bitcoin, and would therefore be detectable by any member of the public.

READ FIRST. This seal anchors a budget-execution record as the finance authority issued it. It does not verify that the underlying expenditures were lawful, fair, or non-corrupt. Public-spending records have a documented history of corruption upstream of issuance — Brazil's Operação Lava Jato documented systematic kickbacks on contracts that appeared routine at the moment of award. A sealed corrupt expenditure is a permanent public record of the corruption, not a defense of it. See FACT 01 and the project's kit README for the full list of failure modes outside the seal's domain.

How to Verify

  1. Download all 8 fact files from facts/.
  2. Recompute each file's SHA-256 and confirm it matches the per-fact hash in build/leaf_hashes.txt.
  3. Compute the Merkle root (algorithm in SPEC §3), or run verify.py.
  4. Verify the OpenTimestamps receipt against Bitcoin: ots verify merkle_root.txt.ots (with both merkle_root.txt and merkle_root.txt.ots present in the same directory).
  5. Independent IPFS check: ipfs add -r --cid-version=1 -Q site-dir/ and confirm the resulting CID matches.
  6. For any supporting document listed by hash in FACT 05: obtain the document via FOI / open-records / direct portal download, compute SHA-256 of the received file, and confirm bit-for-bit identity with the sealed hash.

What this protocol promises (and doesn't)

The protocol delivers detectability of tampering, not impossibility of tampering. It delivers content-addressed mirroring at the IPFS layer, but not guaranteed long-term pinning durability without additional pin redundancy. It does not assert that any sealed fact is true; it asserts only that the fact was published in its current bytewise form at the sealed time.

Specific to this kit: the seal does NOT verify that any expenditure was lawful, that any vendor selection was free of corruption, that any disbursement was for work actually performed at the value claimed, that vendor entity names are not fronts for undisclosed beneficial owners, or that the publication includes all transactions that should have been sealed. The seal anchors WHAT WAS REPORTED. Investigation of WHAT WAS TRUE is the jurisdiction of the audit, inspector-general, prosecutorial, journalistic, and judicial systems.

See the project's threat model and this kit's README for the full list of what the protocol does and does not defend against.
