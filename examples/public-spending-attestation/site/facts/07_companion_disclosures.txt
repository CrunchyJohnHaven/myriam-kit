FACT 07 — COMPANION DISCLOSURES AND REFUSAL RECORD (FICTIONAL)

Per the MYRIAM kit's "constraint-first" design principle, this fact
records what the kit explicitly refused to seal and any companion
disclosures the issuing authority is obliged to publish alongside the
expenditure record so that selective omission is detectable.

In public finance the dominant attack on the integrity of disclosure is
NOT outright fabrication — it is selective publication: posting the
clean transactions, omitting the messy ones, omitting the disputes,
omitting the open investigations. Sealing the companion-disclosure
state alongside the transactions makes selective omission detectable as
a deviation from the as-sealed record.

REFUSAL RECORD — patterns the kit refused to seal

The kit does NOT seal the following types of facts, even if requested
by the issuing authority:

  R1. INDIVIDUAL EMPLOYEE OR OFFICER PII
      No fact in this publication names individual government employees
      below the principal-signing level (FACT 08), individual vendor
      employees, individual bidder representatives, individual scoring
      panel members, or any natural person not occupying a public-
      facing accountability role.

  R2. SUPPLIER COMMERCIAL PII
      The kit refuses to seal supplier bank account numbers, internal
      pricing breakdowns containing supplier cost structure, supplier
      employee lists, supplier tax IDs of natural persons, supplier
      banking routing detail, or anything else whose publication would
      damage commercial interests without serving anti-corruption.
      Such information may be HASHED (see FACT 05) but not published.

  R3. SECURITY-CLASSIFIED EXPENDITURES
      Expenditures classified under national-security statutes are NOT
      included in this publication. A separate sealing regime under
      classified handling is a different problem; this kit refuses to
      handle it. The exclusion itself is disclosed here so that the
      reader knows the publication is incomplete by design rather than
      by oversight.

  R4. CIVIL ENFORCEMENT OF TAX OBLIGATIONS AGAINST IDENTIFIED NATURAL
      PERSONS
      Even though tax-collection actions are public expenditures (of
      enforcement resources), naming individual taxpayers under
      enforcement is refused — the privacy harm exceeds the
      transparency benefit, and parallel transparency exists under
      tax-court reporting rather than spending-attestation.

  R5. CLAIMS THAT THE EXPENDITURE WAS LAWFUL, FAIR, OR CORRUPTION-FREE
      The kit refuses to seal any framing of the form "this expenditure
      was free of corruption" or "the procurement was demonstrably
      fair." Such claims are epistemically beyond the kit's domain. The
      kit anchors WHAT WAS RECORDED; it does not vouch for the
      underlying transaction.

  R6. PRE-ISSUANCE TRANSACTIONS
      The kit refuses to seal contracts before they are formally
      awarded, or disbursements before they are formally issued.
      Anchoring a draft contract or a pending payment as if it were
      executed creates false certainty.

  R7. SEALING BY ANYONE OTHER THAN THE RESPONSIBLE FINANCE PRINCIPAL
      A contractor, vendor, council member, journalist, or third-party
      observer cannot seal "the public spending record" in lieu of the
      finance authority that issued it. (Such third parties may seal
      their OWN observations or investigations under their own
      principal, with their own refusal record, in a separate
      publication.)

  R8. SELECTIVE SEALING THAT OMITS COMPANION DISCLOSURES C1–C5 ABOVE
      The kit refuses to seal a publication that includes FACT 04
      (transactions) but omits FACT 07 (disclosures and refusals).
      A publication without companion disclosures is not a legitimate
      MYRIAM publication under this kit.

If during issuance any request matching R1–R8 was made and refused,
that refusal would be documented as a sealed sub-fact:
  "Refusal #N: party X requested sealing of [pattern]; the kit refused
  per refusal-rule R[m]."

This fact asserts only the companion-disclosure status and refusal
record at the moment of attestation. It does not assert that the
disclosure list is exhaustive of all material companion facts that
SHOULD have been disclosed, that the refusal list is exhaustive of
all attempted misuses of the kit, or that absence of a disclosure
implies absence of the underlying condition. A more honest seal
discloses more; the seal cannot compel honesty about what is not
disclosed.
