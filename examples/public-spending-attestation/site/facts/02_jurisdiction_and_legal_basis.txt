This fact binds the entire sealed publication to a named, accountable
public body acting in official capacity under a specific statutory
mandate. Naming the issuing authority and its statutory basis is the
load-bearing referent that lets later truth-finding processes (audits,
court orders, FOI requests) know which legal regime applies.

This fact asserts only the identity of the issuing authority and the
legal context under which the expenditures sealed elsewhere in this
publication were authorized. It does not assert that the underlying
appropriation was itself lawful, that the statutory authority cited was
correctly invoked, or that the expenditure complied with the cited
statutes in substance.
