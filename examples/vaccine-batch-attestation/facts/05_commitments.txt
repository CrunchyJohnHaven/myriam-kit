FACT 05 — PHARMACOVIGILANCE AND RECALL COMMITMENTS (FICTIONAL)

In the spirit of the MYRIAM protocol's "minimize preventable deaths
over time" objective function, the fictional manufacturer commits, at
the moment of release, to the following:

  1. ADVERSE-EVENT TRANSPARENCY
     All serious adverse events temporally associated with batch
     FBP-2026-Q2-0184 will be reported to the appropriate regulatory
     authorities within statutorily required timelines AND aggregate
     adverse-event counts will be published in a NEW sealed MYRIAM
     publication (referencing this one by Merkle root) at the 6-month
     and 12-month post-release marks.

  2. RECALL EXECUTION
     If a serious safety signal is identified for this batch, the
     manufacturer commits to:
       - Issuing a recall notice within 72 hours of regulator
         concurrence on the signal
       - Publishing the recall scope (which lots, which destinations,
         which dose ranges) as a NEW sealed MYRIAM publication
       - Maintaining recall completeness reporting until ≥98% of
         distributed doses are accounted for

  3. NO SILENT RETESTING
     If retesting of any release test in FACT 03 is performed after
     this release, the retest result will be published as a NEW
     sealed MYRIAM publication regardless of result. Replacement
     of a sealed PASS with an unsealed retest is not a legitimate
     amendment.

  4. STABILITY UPDATES
     Stability time-point results (3, 6, 9, 12 months) for this batch
     will be sealed and published as NEW MYRIAM publications when
     finalized.

  5. NO POST-HOC PROTOCOL CHANGES
     If the release-specification limits in FACT 03 are revised by the
     sponsor or regulator after this release, the revised limits will
     be sealed as a NEW publication. The original sealed limits remain
     the basis on which this batch was released.

This fact asserts only the commitments as declared. It does not assert
that the commitments will be honored in practice, or that honoring them
is sufficient for any specific safety guarantee.

The commitments are a forensic anchor. Any future deviation by the
manufacturer becomes publicly comparable against this sealed declaration.
