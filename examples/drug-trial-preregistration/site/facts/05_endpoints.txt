FACT 05 — PRIMARY AND SECONDARY ENDPOINTS (FICTIONAL)

PRIMARY ENDPOINT (one and only one — singular by design)

  Change from baseline in [Fictional Symptom Severity Score, FSSS]
  at Week 12 (end of treatment).

  - Measured as: ((FSSS_week12) − (FSSS_baseline))
  - Direction of benefit: negative change (decrease) is beneficial
  - Primary analysis: ANCOVA with treatment as fixed effect, baseline
    FSSS as covariate, stratification factors as fixed effects, at
    alpha = 0.05 (two-sided)
  - Missing-data handling: multiple imputation under MAR assumption,
    100 imputations, Rubin's combining rules

KEY SECONDARY ENDPOINTS (pre-ordered for hierarchical testing)

  1. Response rate at Week 12 (proportion with ≥50% reduction in FSSS)
  2. Time to response (Kaplan-Meier, log-rank test)
  3. Change from baseline in [Fictional Quality-of-Life Index, FQLI] at Week 12
  4. Change from baseline in FSSS at Week 24 (post-treatment durability)

OTHER PRE-REGISTERED ENDPOINTS (exploratory)

  - Adverse-event rates (any AE, serious AE, AE leading to
    discontinuation)
  - All-cause mortality
  - Cardiovascular events (independently adjudicated by blinded
    committee — protocol-pre-specified MACE composite)
  - Liver function abnormalities (ALT, AST, bilirubin)

HIERARCHICAL TESTING ORDER

  Testing proceeds in the order above for the primary endpoint and
  the four key secondary endpoints. Testing stops at the first
  non-significant result; subsequent endpoints reported descriptively.
  This controls family-wise error rate at alpha = 0.05.

This fact asserts only the endpoint definitions and testing strategy
as pre-registered. It does not assert that the endpoints capture all
clinically meaningful outcomes, that the testing strategy is the only
defensible one, or that any specific result will be obtained.

CRITICAL NOTE: any post-hoc endpoint substitution (e.g., promoting an
exploratory endpoint to primary after the primary endpoint fails) will
be DETECTABLE as a deviation from this sealed pre-registration. This
is the central protection the seal provides.
