FACT 06 — STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN (FICTIONAL)

SAMPLE SIZE CALCULATION

  Assumed effect size:        0.4 standardized units on FSSS
  Assumed standard deviation: 1.6 units (from prior Phase 2 data)
  Two-sided alpha:            0.05
  Power:                      0.90
  Drop-out adjustment:        20% (assumed)
  Resulting per-arm size:     240
  Total:                      480

PRIMARY ANALYSIS

  Model:           ANCOVA
  Dependent var:   Change in FSSS from baseline to Week 12
  Fixed effects:   Treatment (FICT-X-7 vs placebo); baseline FSSS
                   (continuous covariate); stratification factor
                   (categorical)
  Test:            Two-sided t-test of treatment effect at alpha = 0.05
  Effect estimate: Adjusted mean difference with 95% CI

INTERIM ANALYSES

  Planned interim:  ONE pre-planned interim analysis at 50% enrollment
                    (240 randomized participants), for futility only
                    (no early efficacy stopping). Alpha spending:
                    O'Brien-Fleming, with no alpha spent at interim
                    (futility only; non-binding).
  Futility rule:    Conditional power < 10% under current trend leads
                    to recommendation to DSMB; final decision by DSMB.
  Other looks:      None. No alpha adjustment for unplanned looks.

HANDLING OF MISSING DATA

  Primary:    Multiple imputation (m=100) under Missing At Random
              assumption; Rubin's rules for pooling estimates.
  Sensitivity: Tipping-point analysis (delta-adjustment) to assess
              robustness to MNAR.
  Per-protocol: Complete-case analysis; no imputation.

PROTOCOL DEVIATIONS

  Major deviations are pre-defined in the protocol and will be reported
  in the published trial report. mITT analysis includes participants
  with major deviations; per-protocol analysis excludes them.

OUTPUT REPORTING

  Primary endpoint will be reported regardless of statistical
  significance. The trial sponsor commits, in FACT 08, NOT to suppress
  publication of negative or null results. Pre-registered exploratory
  analyses will also be reported regardless of outcome.

This fact asserts only the statistical analysis plan as pre-registered.
It does not assert that the plan is the only defensible one or that
the assumptions (effect size, SD, dropout rate) are correct.

CRITICAL NOTE: any deviation from this plan in the final analysis
(e.g., changing the test, the imputation method, or the model) will be
DETECTABLE as a deviation from this sealed pre-registration. Deviations
required by unforeseen events (e.g., unexpected enrollment patterns,
discovery of analysis errors) should be documented in a NEW sealed
publication that references this one by Merkle root, BEFORE the primary
analysis is conducted.
