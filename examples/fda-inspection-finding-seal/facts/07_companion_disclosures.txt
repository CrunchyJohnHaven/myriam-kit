REFUSAL RECORD — patterns the kit refused to seal

The kit does NOT seal the following types of facts, even if requested
by the inspection team or supervisory chain:

  R1. PATIENT PII OR TRIAL-SUBJECT IDENTIFIERS
      Adverse-event cases cited in the inspection (e.g. specific
      complaints in Observation 4) may reference identifiable
      patients. Patient names, dates of birth, addresses, insurance
      IDs, and clinical-trial subject IDs MUST NOT be sealed. Only
      de-identified case references (e.g. "complaint #FICT-2025-1042")
      are sealable.

  R2. WHISTLEBLOWER IDENTITY
      The inspection was prompted in part by information from a
      former employee who requested confidentiality protection. The
      whistleblower's name, role, dates of employment, identifying
      pattern of allegations, or anything else that could re-identify
      them MUST NOT be sealed. (The whistleblower has their own
      protected channel under 21 USC and OSC regulations.)

  R3. TRADE-SECRET FORMULAS OR MANUFACTURING PROCESSES
      Quantitative formula details, proprietary process parameters
      (e.g. precise compression force, granulation endpoint moisture),
      and validation-batch process recipes MUST NOT be sealed
      verbatim. The seal references such documents only by SHA-256
      hash (see FACT 04), which proves the document existed without
      exposing its content.

  R4. PARTIAL FINDINGS DURING ONGOING INSPECTION
      The kit refuses to seal a Form 483 mid-inspection. Sealing is
      permitted ONLY at the closing meeting, when all observations
      are finalized. (Defeats the failure mode where an inspector
      under pressure publishes a partial finding that is later
      withdrawn before the full inspection concludes.)

  R5. SEALING BY ANYONE OTHER THAN THE ACTUAL INVESTIGATOR
      The kit refuses to seal a Form 483 at the request of any party
      other than the lead investigator (or, by explicit delegation,
      a member of the inspection team named in FACT 02). A District
      Director, Center reviewer, or anyone else seeking to seal an
      inspection finding must do so as a SEPARATE publication
      attributed to themselves — not under the investigator's
      authority.

  R6. CLASSIFICATIONS NOT SUPPORTED BY THE OBSERVATIONS
      The kit refuses to seal a classification (FACT 05) that is
      inconsistent with the observations in FACT 03 — e.g. NAI
      classification when material observations were issued, or OAI
      classification when no observations were issued. The seal is
      a record of what was found, not a coercion tool for forcing
      a particular regulatory outcome.

  R7. COMPELLED ATTESTATIONS
      The kit refuses to seal under coercion. If the inspector is
      being pressured (by firm counsel, by FDA superiors, or by any
      external party) to issue a Form 483 with different language or
      a different classification than the inspector's own findings
      support, the kit's correct use is to REFUSE to seal AND to
      publish a separate sealed refusal-record describing the
      coercion (without identifying any individual unless that
      individual is a public official acting in public capacity).

If during issuance any request matching R1-R7 was made and refused,
that refusal is documented as a sealed fact:
  "Refusal #N: party X requested sealing of [pattern]; the kit
  refused per refusal-rule R[m]."

  Refusals this issuance: NONE. (FICTIONAL)

This fact asserts only the companion-disclosure status and any
refusal record at the moment of Form 483 issuance. It does not
assert that the refusal-rule list is exhaustive of all PII or
trade-secret patterns, that the companion-disclosure inventory
captures every pending legal matter at the establishment, or that
the inspection team's awareness of parallel FDA activity was complete.
