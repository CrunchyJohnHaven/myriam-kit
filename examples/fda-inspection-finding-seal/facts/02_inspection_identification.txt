This fact binds the entire sealed publication to a specific FDA
inspection of a specific FDA-registered establishment, conducted by
named federal investigators acting in official capacity. Naming the
investigators is appropriate and protective: they are public officials
in their public roles, and the seal makes their inspection finding
publicly attributable in a way that resists later "the investigator
must have been confused" reframing.

This fact asserts only the identity of the establishment, the
inspection metadata, and the investigator team for the fictional
inspection. It does not assert that any real inspection or
establishment exists, nor that the fictional Form 483 reflects any
actual FDA finding.
