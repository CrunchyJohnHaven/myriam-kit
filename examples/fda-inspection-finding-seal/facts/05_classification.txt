FACT 05 — PRELIMINARY INSPECTION CLASSIFICATION (FICTIONAL)

This fact records the lead investigator's PRELIMINARY classification of
the inspection at the moment of closing-meeting issuance. The FDA's
final classification is determined later by the District Office and the
Center compliance evaluation; that final classification may agree with
or differ from the preliminary one. Sealing the preliminary classification
makes any later silent downgrade — for example, an "OAI" finding at
closing being silently reclassified to "VAI" before EIR publication —
publicly detectable.

THREE FDA INSPECTION CLASSIFICATIONS

  NAI — "No Action Indicated":      No objectionable conditions or
                                    practices were found during the
                                    inspection (or the conditions are
                                    de minimis).
  VAI — "Voluntary Action Indicated": Objectionable conditions were
                                    found, but they do not meet the
                                    threshold for regulatory or
                                    administrative action; firm is
                                    expected to correct voluntarily.
  OAI — "Official Action Indicated":  Conditions warrant regulatory or
                                    administrative action — Warning
                                    Letter, Consent Decree, Application
                                    Integrity Policy invocation, recall
                                    request, seizure, or injunction.

PRELIMINARY CLASSIFICATION (FICTIONAL)

  Classification at closing:    OAI

WHY SEAL THIS AT CLOSING (NOT AT EIR PUBLICATION)

In ordinary FDA process, the District Office and Center reviewers
evaluate the inspection findings between the closing meeting and EIR
publication. That review is legitimate and necessary — investigators
sometimes miscite, miscount, or misclassify; the review catches that.

The failure mode this seal defends against is NOT legitimate
post-inspection review. It is: silent reclassification that no
investigator endorsed, made AFTER the closing meeting, that is
attributed to "the inspection team's final determination" when in fact
the investigators were not consulted on the change.

A sealed preliminary classification gives the investigator forensic
recourse if their classification is later silently changed without
their knowledge. The investigator can publish a follow-up sealed fact
saying "the published EIR classifies this inspection as VAI; my
preliminary classification at the closing meeting on 2026-05-21 was
OAI; I was not consulted on the change."

POST-ISSUANCE COMMITMENTS BY THE INVESTIGATOR (declared at issuance)

  1. If the District Office or Center compliance reviewer changes the
     classification, the lead investigator commits to documenting the
     change in a memo to the file referencing this sealed preliminary
     classification by Merkle root.

  2. If the EIR-published version of any observation in FACT 03 differs
     from the verbatim text sealed in FACT 03 by anything other than
     spelling, formatting, or pagination, the lead investigator commits
     to noting the discrepancy in a memo to the file referencing the
     sealed fact by Merkle root.

  3. If the EIR is silently delayed beyond the FDA's internal target of
     45 days for OAI inspections, the lead investigator commits to
     filing a memo noting the delay.

These commitments do not bind the District Office, the Center, or the
Agency — only the individual investigator, who retains the right at
any later moment to file a NEW sealed publication describing what was
changed in their inspection finding and by whom.

This fact asserts only the preliminary classification as it stood at
the moment of closing-meeting issuance and the investigator's
post-issuance commitments. It does not assert that this classification
is the FDA's final determination, that the firm cannot rebut it, or
that a NAI classification of any inspection is in error simply because
some observation existed. It defers all final adjudication to the
formal EIR process and any subsequent enforcement action.
