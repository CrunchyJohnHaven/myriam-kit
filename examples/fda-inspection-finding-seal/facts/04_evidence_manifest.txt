FACT 04 — EVIDENCE MANIFEST (FICTIONAL)

This fact records the SHA-256 hashes of the photographs, document
copies, and sample collection records that the inspection team
gathered during the inspection identified in FACT 02. The raw
evidence files are NOT included in this publication (the FDA holds
them under records-control); only their hashes are sealed, so this
attestation remains durable even after the underlying files are
archived to FDA cold storage or transferred between FDA offices.

The point of sealing the evidence hashes at issuance: if any
photograph, document copy, or sample collection record is silently
altered, replaced, or "lost" during later EIR drafting or any
post-issuance review, re-hashing the produced file and comparing it
to the row below will reveal the alteration.

A regulator, the District Office reviewer, OGC counsel, or a future
court can request any individual file behind any hash above. Re-hashing
the produced file with SHA-256 and comparing to the row above verifies
that the file received matches the file that existed at the closing
meeting. Any post-issuance substitution of a softened photograph (e.g.
cropped to remove evidence), a redacted document (e.g. an SOP page
silently replaced with a "current revision"), or a discarded sample
record produces a different hash than the sealed one and is publicly
detectable.

This fact asserts only the SHA-256 hashes of the evidence collected
during the fictional inspection at the moment of closing-meeting
issuance. It does not assert that the evidence is exhaustive of what
should have been collected, that the hashes confer admissibility under
any specific evidentiary regime, or that production of any underlying
file is authorized to any party without the appropriate FDA records
control.
