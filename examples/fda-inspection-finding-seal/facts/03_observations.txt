FACT 03 — FORM 483 INSPECTIONAL OBSERVATIONS (FICTIONAL)

This fact records the verbatim text of the Form FDA-483 Inspectional
Observations issued to the establishment named in FACT 02 at the closing
meeting on the date and time in FACT 02. Each observation cites the
specific 21 CFR section the investigator found the establishment to be
in violation of. Each observation is numbered as issued.

Sealing this fact at the closing-meeting moment prevents the most
common silent-revision failure mode: an observation that was severe at
issuance being softened — or deleted entirely — in a later draft of
the EIR or in a "revised" Form 483 issued after firm pushback.

OBSERVATION 1 (FICTIONAL)
  Citation: 21 CFR 211.192 (Production record review)
  Text: "Failure to thoroughly review unexplained discrepancies in
  batch production. Specifically, batches FBP-2025-Q4-0114,
  FBP-2025-Q4-0119, and FBP-2026-Q1-0027 of Drug A showed
  out-of-specification dissolution results during in-process testing
  that were attributed to 'analyst error' without root-cause
  investigation. The firm released all three batches to distribution
  without invoking the deviation procedure required under SOP-QA-014."

OBSERVATION 2 (FICTIONAL)
  Citation: 21 CFR 211.42(c)(10)(iv) (Sterile manufacturing — environmental
  monitoring)
  Text: "Environmental-monitoring data from the Grade A fill suite for the
  period 2026-01-01 through 2026-03-31 shows 14 excursions above the alert
  level for non-viable particulate (≥0.5 µm) during active aseptic
  processing operations, none of which triggered the documented response
  protocol. The firm's EM trend report dated 2026-04-04 characterizes the
  excursions as 'monitoring artifact' without supporting investigation."

OBSERVATION 3 (FICTIONAL)
  Citation: 21 CFR 211.165(a) (Testing and release for distribution)
  Text: "The firm released to distribution batches of Drug B for which
  the assay test result was reported as a single replicate value when
  the approved release method specifies three replicate determinations
  with reportable result as the mean. Affected batches include
  FBP-2025-Q3-0204 through FBP-2026-Q1-0048 (32 batches over a
  nine-month period). The firm's QC director acknowledged the deviation
  during the inspection on 2026-05-15."

OBSERVATION 4 (FICTIONAL)
  Citation: 21 CFR 211.198 (Complaint files)
  Text: "The firm received 47 complaints during the period
  2025-06-01 through 2026-05-04 alleging foreign particulate matter
  in oral solid dosage product. Of these, 31 were closed without
  investigation as 'cannot be confirmed.' The firm's complaint-handling
  SOP requires sample retention and root-cause investigation for any
  particulate complaint regardless of returnable sample availability."

OBSERVATION 5 (FICTIONAL)
  Citation: 21 CFR 211.68(b) (Automatic, mechanical, and electronic
  equipment — data integrity)
  Text: "The HPLC system in QC Laboratory 3 (instrument ID FQC-HPLC-007)
  was operated under a shared user account with administrative privileges
  during the period 2024-Q1 through 2026-Q2. Audit-trail review during
  the inspection revealed 18 instances of integration-parameter changes
  applied to OOS assay runs without a corresponding deviation record or
  laboratory investigation. The firm could not produce a contemporaneous
  metadata reconstruction for the affected runs."

This fact asserts only the verbatim text and the per-observation hashes
of the Form FDA-483 issued at the closing meeting for the fictional
inspection. It does not assert that the firm has admitted to the
observations, that the citations are themselves correct interpretations
of 21 CFR, or that the observations exhaust all deficiencies that
existed at the establishment. It does not preempt the firm's right to
respond under 21 CFR 10.30 or the FDA's right to amend its inspection
findings through the formal EIR review process.
