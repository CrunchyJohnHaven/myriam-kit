FACT 06 — FIRM RESPONSE WINDOW (FICTIONAL)

This fact establishes — AT THE MOMENT OF FORM 483 ISSUANCE — the
firm's deadline to respond to the inspection observations. Sealing the
window at issuance prevents the most common post-issuance dispute:
"the firm was given X days; the FDA later acted on day Y; what was the
window?"

  Within the response window, the firm is expected to submit, in
  writing, for each observation in FACT 03:
    (a) The firm's response to the observation (concur / partially
        concur / dispute).
    (b) Immediate corrective actions taken or planned.
    (c) Longer-term preventive actions and target completion dates.
    (d) Supporting documentation (revised SOPs, training records,
        investigation reports, capa records).

  Failure-to-respond consequence (per FDA guidance):
    Absence of timely response or substantively inadequate response is
    a factor the District Office and Center weigh in deciding whether
    a Warning Letter or other regulatory action is warranted.

WHY SEAL THE WINDOW AT ISSUANCE

The response-window deadline is a specific calendar date computed at
the moment of issuance. Sealing it prevents two post-hoc disputes:

  D1. SILENT EXTENSION
      "The FDA gave us 30 days, not 15." — Defeated by the sealed
      2026-06-04 deadline.

  D2. SILENT TRUNCATION
      "The FDA acted before our response window closed." — Defeated
      by the sealed 2026-06-04 deadline being public.

POST-RESPONSE COMMITMENTS

  C1. Once the firm submits its written response, that response is
      expected to be entered into the inspection record. A NEW sealed
      MYRIAM publication (referencing this one by Merkle root) is the
      recommended way for the firm or the investigator to memorialize
      the response so its contents cannot be silently revised later.

  C2. If the District Office grants the firm an extension of the
      response window, the extension memorandum should itself be
      sealed as a NEW publication referencing this one by Merkle root.
      Silent extensions — granted but not documented — become
      detectable as deviations from this sealed window.

  C3. If a Warning Letter is issued, the issuance date and the
      observations cited in the Warning Letter can be compared against
      the observations in FACT 03 of this seal. Any observation cited
      in a Warning Letter that does NOT appear in FACT 03 is a deviation
      from inspection scope and should itself trigger a new sealed
      publication explaining the basis.

This fact asserts only the response-window dates and the
issuance-method details for the fictional Form 483. It does not assert
that 15 working days is the correct response window for every
inspection (it varies by inspection type and Compliance Program), that
the firm will respond at all, or that the FDA will adhere to the
implied 45-day EIR publication target for OAI inspections.
