In a real attestation, the signing principal section should include:

  - Each investigator's PGP public key fingerprint OR equivalent
    cryptographic identity (so later sealed publications by the same
    investigator can be tied to this issuance)
  - The actual FDA District Office name and address
  - The actual Form 483 sequence number assigned by the FDA's
    document-control system
  - The supervisory chain as it existed at issuance (these officers
    may rotate; the sealed snapshot anchors who was accountable
    on issuance day)

The signing principal section SHOULD NOT include:

  - Personal contact information (phone, home address) for
    investigators or supervisory officers
  - Identifying information about investigators' family members
  - FDA employee ID numbers (held under FDA records control;
    not appropriate for public seal)
  - Any information that would create a personal-safety risk for
    federal inspectors, who in some sectors face documented
    intimidation or retaliation pressure

If an investigator is willing to be named on the public seal,
naming them is appropriate (federal office, federal role, federal
inspection act). If an investigator declines to be named — including
because the inspection touches a sector where personal retaliation
risk is real — the seal can still be valid with the inspection team
identified by anonymized investigator ID and the District Office
named as the institutional signatory.

The PGP key (or equivalent) is the load-bearing element of this fact.
Without a per-investigator cryptographic identity, the seal proves
only that "an FDA Form 483 was issued at time T with content X" —
which is useful — but cannot independently prove that any subsequent
sealed publication purporting to be from the same investigator is
genuinely from them. With the key, the investigator retains the
ability across years and across changes in employment to publish
new sealed statements ("the EIR for INS-99-9999999 has been
silently revised; here is what I actually issued") that
cryptographically chain to this issuance.

This fact asserts only the signing principal identification fields
for the fictional Form 483 issuance as declared. It does not assert
any of those fields about any real investigator, supervisory officer,
or District Office.
