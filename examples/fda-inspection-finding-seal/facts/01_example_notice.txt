FACT 01 — EXAMPLE NOTICE

This publication is an EXAMPLE demonstration of how an FDA investigator
(or an investigator's supervisory chain) can use myriam-kit to seal a
Form FDA-483 Inspectional Observations attestation at the moment of
issuance. The establishment, FEI number, inspection dates, investigators,
observations, evidence hashes, and classification are ALL FICTIONAL.

The purpose: show the SHAPE of a real Form 483 issuance seal. A real
attestation would replace each fact body with the actual content of an
actual closing-meeting Form 483.

This kit is intended to compose with — not replace — the FDA's existing
inspection infrastructure (the Form 483 itself, the Establishment
Inspection Report (EIR), the FDA district office review chain, the
Center compliance evaluation, and any subsequent Warning Letter,
Consent Decree, or Application Integrity Policy invocation). The MYRIAM
seal provides Bitcoin-anchored tamper-evidence on top of whatever
internal FDA review the inspection already triggers.

The seal is issued AT THE CLOSING MEETING — the moment the Form 483 is
formally presented to the firm's most-responsible person. A seal issued
earlier (during the inspection, before all observations are finalized)
is refused by the kit per FACT 07.

This fact asserts only that the publication is an example, not a real
Form 483 issuance. No real inspection is hereby memorialized. No real
investigator, firm, or product is hereby named.
